# Alpari Review 2026: Is This Fallen Giant Still Worth Trading With?


---

## Overview

Alpari is one of the most recognizable names in retail forex trading, with a brand history stretching back to 1998. Originally founded in Kazan, Russia, by Andrey Dashin, the company grew into a global operation spanning the UK, US, Russia, Cyprus, and multiple offshore jurisdictions. At its peak, Alpari sponsored West Ham United Football Club and was one of the most visible retail forex brokers in the world.

However, the brand's trajectory changed dramatically on January 15, 2015, when the Swiss National Bank unexpectedly removed its EUR/CHF floor. The resulting market chaos exposed fundamental weaknesses in Alpari's risk management, leading to the insolvency of its UK entity and the subsequent revocation of licenses across multiple jurisdictions.

Today, Alpari operates under a fundamentally different structure. Separated from the Exinity Group (which also owns FXTM) in July 2025, the brand now operates through Parlance Trading Ltd, registered in Mohéli, Comoros, with a license from the Mwali International Services Authority (MISA T2023236).

In this detailed 2026 review, we'll examine whether Alpari's brand recognition and low entry barriers can compensate for its dramatic regulatory downgrade and history of insolvency.

**Quick Verdict:** Alpari is not recommended for traders with access to properly regulated alternatives. The brand's only active regulation is from the Comoros Islands (MISA), a tier-3 offshore regulator offering minimal investor protection. The $5 minimum deposit and 1:3000 leverage may seem attractive, but they come with significant risk.

### What This Means For You

**The elephant in the room:** Alpari currently operates without ANY tier-1 regulatory oversight. The brand's only active license comes from the Comoros Islands (Mwali International Services Authority), which is considered a tier-3 offshore regulator with minimal investor protection standards.

**Critical risk factors:**

- 🚨 **UK entity went bankrupt** in January 2015 after the Swiss Franc crisis, owing clients significant funds
- 🚨 **Russian license annulled** by the Bank of Russia for "repeated violations of securities law"
- 🚨 **US NFA membership revoked** in April 2015
- 🚨 **Multiple license revocations** across FCA, NFA, CBR, CySEC, and IFSC
- 🚨 **Listed on RBI "alert list"** — India's Reserve Bank of India flagged Alpari as unauthorized (September 2022)
- 🚨 **Corporate instability** — Brand changed corporate structure multiple times (Alpari Group → Exinity → Parlance Trading Ltd)

**What Alpari still offers:**
- ⚠️ Operating since 1998 — 25+ years of brand history
- ⚠️ Low minimum deposit — Just $5 on Standard accounts
- ⚠️ High leverage — Up to 1:3000 on international accounts

**Risk Level: HIGH**

Alpari's sole MISA Comoros license provides minimal investor protection. The broker lacks access to any investor compensation scheme, and the history of insolvency in the UK means there is precedent for the company failing to meet client obligations.
