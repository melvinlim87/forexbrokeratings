## Understanding CFD Costs

### Spreads vs Commission Models

CFD brokers use two main pricing models:

1. **Spread-only** (e.g., Plus500, XM) — All costs built into the spread. Simple but potentially wider.
2. **Raw spread + commission** (e.g., IC Markets, FP Markets) — Tighter spreads but commission added on top.

**Which is cheaper?** For active traders, raw spread + commission is almost always cheaper. For occasional traders, spread-only is simpler to understand.

### Hidden Costs to Watch

- **Overnight financing** — Daily swap charges for positions held overnight
- **Inactivity fees** — Some brokers charge after 6-12 months of no trading
- **Currency conversion fees** — If your account currency differs from the instrument
- **Guaranteed stop-loss premiums** — Some brokers charge extra for GSLOs
- **Dividend adjustments** — Stock CFD holders receive adjusted dividends (or pay them)

## CFD Regulation: What You Need to Know

CFD trading is regulated differently around the world:

| Region | Max Retail Leverage | Key Regulator | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|-------|
| **UK** | 1:30 (forex) | FCA | Gold standard; negative balance protection required |
| **EU** | 1:30 (forex) | CySEC (per MiFID II) | ESMA rules apply across EU |
| **Australia** | 1:30 (forex) | ASIC | Recently tightened CFD rules |
| **International** | Up to 1:500+ | Various (FSA, IFSC, SCB) | Higher leverage but less protection |

**Key takeaway:** Always prioritize regulation over leverage. A well-regulated broker with 1:30 leverage is safer than an unregulated broker offering 1:500.

## Risks of CFD Trading

CFDs are high-risk instruments. Consider these warnings seriously:

- **70-85% of retail accounts lose money** when trading CFDs (per regulator data)
- Leverage magnifies both gains AND losses
- You can lose more than your initial deposit with some brokers (without negative balance protection)
- Market gaps and volatility can cause slippage beyond expected levels
- CFDs are not suitable for long-term investing due to overnight financing costs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best CFD broker for beginners?

**Plus500** is the best CFD broker for beginners due to its clean interface, zero commissions, and comprehensive demo account. **XM Group** is an excellent alternative for those wanting to start with real money at just $5 minimum deposit.

### Which CFD broker has the lowest spreads?

**IC Markets** and **FP Markets** both offer raw spreads starting from 0.0 pips on major forex pairs. For stock CFDs, **IG** and **CMC Markets** offer competitive pricing.

### Are CFD brokers regulated?

Reputable CFD brokers are regulated by authorities like the FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), or CySEC (Cyprus). Always verify a broker's regulatory status before depositing funds. Unregulated CFD brokers should be avoided entirely.

### What's the maximum leverage for CFD trading?

Maximum leverage depends on your jurisdiction and regulatory framework. In the UK/EU, retail traders are limited to 1:30 on major forex pairs. In less regulated jurisdictions, leverage up to 1:500 is common — but higher leverage means higher risk.

### Can I lose more than I deposit trading CFDs?

Without negative balance protection, yes. However, FCA and ASIC-regulated brokers are required to provide negative balance protection, meaning you cannot lose more than your account balance. Always verify this protection is in place.
