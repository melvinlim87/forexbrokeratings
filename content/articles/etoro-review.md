## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Is eToro a legitimate and safe broker?
Yes. eToro is regulated by the FCA (FRN 583263), CySEC (109/10), ASIC (AFSL 491139), and is publicly traded on NASDAQ (ETOR) since May 2025. It has 38M+ users and 18+ years of operating history.

### 2. What is the minimum deposit at eToro?
The minimum deposit varies by country, typically ranging from $10 to $200. Most countries require $50-$100. Check eToro's website for your specific region.

### 3. Can I actually make money with copy trading?
Copy trading can be profitable, but it's not guaranteed. eToro's Popular Investors have verifiable track records, but past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Diversifying across multiple traders and monitoring performance is recommended.

### 4. What are eToro's fees for forex trading?
eToro charges no commission on forex, but spreads are wider than ECN brokers. EUR/USD averages approximately 1.0 pips. There's also a 0.5% currency conversion fee unless you open an account in your local currency.

### 5. Does eToro charge withdrawal fees?
Yes. eToro charges $5 per withdrawal. Platinum tier members ($25,000+ equity) and above have withdrawal fees waived.

### 6. Can US residents use eToro?
Yes, but with limitations. US clients can trade real stocks, ETFs, and cryptocurrencies. CFD and forex trading are not available for US residents due to regulatory restrictions.

### 7. What is the currency conversion fee?
eToro charges 0.5% for currency conversion when depositing or withdrawing in a currency different from your account currency. You can avoid this by opening an account in your local currency (GBP, EUR available).

### 8. Does eToro offer a demo account?
Yes. eToro provides a free demo account with $100,000 in virtual funds. You can practice trading and test copy trading strategies risk-free.
