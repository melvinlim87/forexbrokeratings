### ⚠️ Critical Regulatory History

Alpari's regulatory journey is the most important factor in any assessment. In January 2015, the Swiss National Bank unexpectedly removed the EUR/CHF peg, causing the "Black Swan" event. Alpari UK suffered massive losses leading to insolvency. The FCA subsequently revoked its license (#441168) in July 2015 — a major credibility hit that fundamentally changed this broker's risk profile.

The current Alpari International operates under St. Vincent and the Grenadines (SVG) FSA registration. It's critical to understand that **SVG does not regulate forex brokers** — the SVGFSA has explicitly stated this. The SVG registration is merely a company incorporation, not authorization to provide financial services.

## FAQ

### Is Alpari regulated?
Alpari operates primarily under SVG FSA registration (IBC #21498), which is not actual forex regulation. Its FCA license was revoked in 2015. Russian clients are served under CBR regulation.

### Can I withdraw my money from Alpari?
Withdrawal experiences are mixed. While many clients withdraw successfully, recent TrustPilot reviews (2025-2026) report delays of weeks and disappearing withdrawal buttons. Crypto withdrawals appear more reliable than bank transfers.

### What is the minimum deposit for Alpari?
$50 for Standard accounts, $300 for ECN, and $500 for Pro ECN.

### What happened to Alpari UK?
Alpari UK was declared insolvent in January 2015 after the Swiss Franc crisis caused massive losses. Its FCA license (#441168) was revoked in July 2015.
