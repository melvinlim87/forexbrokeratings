## Executive Summary

HYCM (Henyep Capital Markets) is one of the longest-running names in retail forex, tracing its origins back to 1977 when the Henyep Group established HY Markets in Hong Kong. Rebranded to HYCM in 2014, the broker holds licenses from the FCA (UK, #185223), CySEC (Cyprus, #259/14), DFSA (Dubai, #F003684), and CIMA (Cayman Islands). With over 48 years of operational history, HYCM offers rare institutional credibility in the retail space. Its Raw account delivers EUR/USD spreads from 0.20 pips, and execution speed claims an impressive 12ms average. However, the $50 withdrawal fee, $10/month inactivity charge after just 90 days, and limited platform selection (MT4/MT5 only) drag down its overall value proposition.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is HYCM a legitimate broker?

Yes. HYCM has been operating since 1977 and holds licenses from the FCA (UK, #185223), CySEC (#259/14), DFSA (Dubai, #F003684), and CIMA (Cayman Islands). It's part of the Henyep Group, a diversified financial services conglomerate based in Hong Kong.

### What is the minimum deposit for HYCM?

$100 for Fixed and Classic accounts, $200 for the Raw account.

### What are HYCM's spreads?

Raw account: from 0.20 pips (EUR/USD) + $4/side commission. Classic: from 1.2 pips with no commission. Fixed: from 1.8 pips.

### Why does HYCM charge a $50 withdrawal fee?

HYCM has not publicly justified this fee. It's significantly higher than the industry standard of $0 and is one of the most common complaints in user reviews. Consider consolidating withdrawals to minimize this cost.

### Does HYCM allow Expert Advisors?

Yes on Classic and Raw accounts. EAs are NOT available on the Fixed Spread account — a notable restriction for algorithmic traders.

## Verdict

HYCM's greatest strength is its pedigree. Nearly five decades of operation, FCA regulation since 1998, and a spotless regulatory record give it credibility that few brokers can match. The 12ms execution speed is genuinely impressive, and Raw spreads on EUR/USD are competitive. But the $50 withdrawal fee is a significant drawback that will cost active traders real money over time, and the 90-day inactivity window is the shortest we've seen. If you can look past the fee structure, HYCM is a solid, stable choice — especially for Middle East-based traders. If fees matter more than pedigree, competitors like Pepperstone or IC Markets offer better overall value.

**Final Rating: ⭐⭐⭐½ (3.5/5) — Good pedigree and execution, but fee structure needs modernizing.**
