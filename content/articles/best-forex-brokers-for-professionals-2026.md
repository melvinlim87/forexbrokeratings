## What Defines a "Professional" Forex Broker?

Not every broker with a "Pro" account tier actually delivers professional-grade service. Here's what we looked for:

### Direct Market Access (DMA)

DMA means your orders go directly to the order book of liquidity providers without broker intervention. No dealing desk, no "last look" delays, no requotes. For professional traders, DMA isn't a luxury — it's a requirement.

### API Access

Professional traders need programmatic access to the market. Whether you're building custom trading algorithms, connecting third-party analytics tools, or managing multiple accounts through a unified interface, comprehensive API access is non-negotiable.

### Professional Account Tiers

True professional accounts offer higher leverage (up to 500:1), negative balance protection opt-out (where permitted by regulation), dedicated account managers, institutional-grade reporting, and access to interbank liquidity pools.

### Multi-Asset Capabilities

Professional traders don't just trade forex. They need access to indices, commodities, bonds, stocks, options, and futures — ideally from a single platform with unified margin and portfolio analytics.

### Institutional-Grade Infrastructure

This means co-located servers, fiber-optic connections to liquidity providers, FIX protocol support, and 99.99% uptime guarantees.

## Professional Client Classification: What It Means

### EU MiFID II Professional Client

Under MiFID II, traders can apply for "elective professional client" status if they meet at least two of three criteria:

1. **Trading volume:** 10 significant transactions per quarter over the last four quarters
2. **Portfolio size:** Financial instrument portfolio exceeding €500,000
3. **Professional experience:** Worked in the financial sector for at least one year in a professional position

Benefits of professional classification:
- Higher leverage (up to 500:1 vs 30:1 for retail)
- Access to professional-only products
- Dedicated account management
- Institutional-grade research

Trade-offs:
- You lose negative balance protection (in some jurisdictions)
- Reduced regulatory compensation scheme coverage
- Less regulatory disclosure requirements for the broker

### US Accredited/Eligible Contract Participant

In the US, professional trading status falls under "Eligible Contract Participant" (ECP) definitions under the Commodity Exchange Act. Generally, this requires:
- $10 million in assets (for individuals)
- OR qualifying corporate/partnership structures
