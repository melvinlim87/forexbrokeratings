## What to Look for Beyond Minimum Deposit

### Micro Lot Trading (0.01 Lots)

A low minimum deposit is useless without micro lot support. At 0.01 lots (1,000 units), each pip movement equals $0.10. This lets you manage risk properly with a small account — risking only $1–$3 per trade with a 10–30 pip stop loss. All brokers on our list support 0.01 lot minimums.

### No Hidden Fees on Small Accounts

Some brokers lure traders with low minimums but charge inactivity fees ($10–$25/month), withdrawal fees ($20–$30), or widen spreads on small accounts. We specifically tested each broker with $10–$100 deposits to verify there are no hidden costs.

### Proper Regulation

A $5 minimum deposit means nothing if the broker isn't regulated. Unregulated brokers can disappear with your deposit, manipulate prices, or refuse withdrawals. Every broker on our list is regulated by at least one Tier-1 or Tier-2 financial authority.

### Fractional Pip Pricing

Small accounts need precise execution. Fractional pip pricing (5 decimal places on EUR/USD) means your order fills at exact prices rather than rounded to whole pips. This matters more for small accounts where every fraction of a pip counts.

## How to Trade Forex with a Small Account

### Rule 1: Risk No More Than 1% Per Trade

With a $100 account, your maximum risk per trade should be $1.00. At 0.01 lots (micro), EUR/USD moves $0.10 per pip, so a 10-pip stop loss risks $1.00. This keeps you in the game long enough to develop consistent strategies.

### Rule 2: Use Micro or Nano Lots

Always choose brokers that offer 0.01 lot (micro) or 0.001 lot (nano) trading. Standard lots (1.0) require $10 per pip — completely inappropriate for small accounts. Micro lots at $0.10/pip and nano lots at $0.01/pip allow proper position sizing.

### Rule 3: Focus on 1–2 Currency Pairs

Don't spread a small account across 20 instruments. Focus on EUR/USD and one other major pair. Learn their behavior, volatility patterns, and optimal trading hours. Depth of knowledge beats breadth of instruments.

### Rule 4: Choose Commission-Free Accounts for Micro Trading

At 0.01 lots, a $7.00/lot commission equals $0.07 per trade. That's 7% of a $1 risk. Commission-free Standard or Micro accounts with wider spreads (1.0–1.5 pips) actually cost less at micro lot sizes because $0.10–$0.15 per trade is cheaper than $0.07 commission + tight spreads.

### Rule 5: Don't Chase High Leverage

Brokers offering 1:1000 or 1:Unlimited leverage are tempting for small accounts, but high leverage magnifies losses as much as gains. Stick to 1:30–1:100 until you have a proven track record.
