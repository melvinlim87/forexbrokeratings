## How We Evaluate ECN Brokers for Singapore

### True ECN Verification Checklist

Not every broker claiming "ECN" actually provides genuine ECN execution. Here's our verification checklist:

1. **Level II Market Depth** — True ECN brokers display the full order book with bid/ask prices from multiple liquidity providers
2. **Commission-Based Pricing** — Genuine ECN brokers charge a commission (typically $3.50–$7.00 per lot per side) rather than marking up spreads
3. **No Dealing Desk** — Orders go directly to liquidity providers without broker intervention
4. **Rejection-Free Execution** — Orders are filled at market price without requotes
5. **Variable Spreads** — True ECN spreads fluctuate with market conditions (tightening to 0.0 pips during liquid hours)

### Singapore-Specific Criteria

- **MAS Regulation** — Preferred but not required if Tier-1 regulated and accepting SG clients
- **SGD Accounts** — Denominate your account in Singapore Dollars to avoid conversion fees
- **Local Deposit Methods** — FAST bank transfers, PayNow, or local card processing
- **Asia-Pacific Server Location** — Singapore, Tokyo, or Sydney servers for lower latency
- **SGD Pairs Available** — USD/SGD with tight spreads

## Understanding MAS Regulation for Forex Trading in Singapore

### What Is MAS?

The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) is Singapore's central bank and financial regulatory authority. MAS regulates forex brokers under the **Securities and Futures Act (SFA)**, which requires:

- Minimum base capital of SGD 1 million (for Capital Markets Services licensees)
- Segregation of client funds in trust accounts
- Regular audits and financial reporting
- Fit and proper assessments for key personnel
- Compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations

### Why MAS Regulation Matters

MAS-regulated brokers offer the strongest protection for Singapore traders:
- **Fund segregation** — your money is held separately from the broker's operating funds
- **Dispute resolution** — access to the Financial Industry Disputes Resolution Centre (FIDReC)
- **Capital adequacy** — brokers must maintain sufficient capital buffers
- **Regular inspections** — MAS conducts on-site inspections of licensed entities

### Can Singapore Traders Use Non-MAS Regulated Brokers?

Yes. Singapore residents can legally open accounts with overseas brokers regulated by other Tier-1 authorities (ASIC, FCA, CySEC). However, you lose the protections of MAS regulation, including FIDReC dispute resolution. For the strongest protection, choose brokers with MAS licenses (IG, Saxo Bank, OANDA, Interactive Brokers, Swissquote).
