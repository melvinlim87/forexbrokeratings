## MT4 Broker Selection Checklist

When choosing an MT4 broker, verify these key points:

- [ ] **Regulation**: At least one Tier-1 license (ASIC, FCA, CFTC, CySEC)
- [ ] **Execution type**: ECN/STP preferred over market maker
- [ ] **Spread on your trading pairs**: Check live spreads, not advertised minimums
- [ ] **Commission structure**: Calculate all-in cost (spread + commission)
- [ ] **EA restrictions**: Confirm scalping, hedging, and news trading are allowed
- [ ] **VPS availability**: Free or low-cost VPS reduces EA latency
- [ ] **Withdrawal speed**: Test with a small withdrawal before depositing large amounts
- [ ] **MT4 plugin support**: MQL4 marketplace, custom indicators, EAs

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is MetaTrader 4 still the best platform for forex trading?

**MT4 remains the most popular platform** for forex trading, especially for algorithmic traders and EA users. Its vast ecosystem of custom indicators, Expert Advisors, and community resources is unmatched. However, MT5 offers more timeframes, order types, and better backtesting. For most retail traders, MT4 is still the go-to choice.

### Can I use the same MT4 EA on multiple brokers?

**Yes.** EAs are platform-specific, not broker-specific. Any MQL4 EA will work on any broker's MT4 installation. However, results will differ between brokers due to variations in spread, execution speed, and slippage. Always forward-test your EA on a demo account with a new broker before going live.

### What's the difference between Standard and Raw MT4 accounts?

**Standard accounts** have wider spreads (0.8–1.6 pips on EUR/USD) but charge no commission. **Raw accounts** offer interbank spreads (0.0–0.3 pips) but charge a commission ($2–$5 per lot). Raw accounts are cheaper for active traders; standard accounts suit those who trade infrequently or with smaller lot sizes.

### Which MT4 broker is best for US traders?

**FOREX.com and OANDA** are the best MT4 brokers for US residents. Due to CFTC regulations, very few international brokers accept US clients. Both FOREX.com and OANDA are CFTC-regulated, NFA-registered, and offer full MT4 access with EA support.

### Do MT4 brokers allow hedging?

**Most international MT4 brokers allow hedging** (simultaneous long and short positions on the same instrument). However, **US-based brokers are required to enforce FIFO (First In, First Out)** rules, which effectively prohibits hedging on US-regulated MT4 accounts. If hedging is important to you, use a non-US regulated broker.

### How much money do I need to start trading on MT4?

**You can start with as little as $0–$200** depending on the broker. OANDA and BlackBull Markets have $0 minimums. Vantage requires just $50. However, responsible risk management suggests starting with at least $500–$1,000 so you can maintain proper position sizing without risking more than 1–2% per trade.
