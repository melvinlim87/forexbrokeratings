For Singaporean traders, choosing an MAS-regulated broker isn't just about compliance — it's about protection. MAS requires brokers to maintain **minimum capital of SGD 1 million**, hold client funds in segregated accounts, and submit to regular audits. If a broker goes under, MAS-licensed client funds are protected differently than in many offshore jurisdictions.

## Understanding MAS Regulation

The Monetary Authority of Singapore regulates forex brokers under the **Securities and Futures Act (SFA)**. Key requirements include:

- **Capital Adequacy**: Minimum SGD 1 million base capital
- **Segregated Funds**: Client money must be held separate from company funds
- **Risk Disclosure**: Brokers must provide clear risk warnings
- **Leverage Limits**: MAS limits retail leverage to **20:1 for major pairs** and **10:1 for minors/exotics**
- **Reporting**: Regular financial reporting and audit requirements

Not all brokers serving Singaporean clients are MAS-regulated. Some operate under offshore licenses (FSA Seychelles, VFSC Vanuatu) while still accepting SG residents. We prioritize MAS-licensed brokers in our rankings.

## MAS Leverage Limits

MAS imposes leverage caps on retail forex traders:

| Pair Type | Max Leverage (Retail) |
|---|---|
| Major pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, etc.) | 20:1 |
| Minor pairs (EUR/GBP, AUD/CAD, etc.) | 10:1 |
| Exotic pairs (USD/TRY, EUR/ZAR, etc.) | 5:1 |
| CFD indices | 20:1 |
| CFD commodities | 10:1 |
| CFD stocks | 5:1 |

Professional clients may qualify for higher leverage (up to 100:1) by meeting specific criteria including trading experience, portfolio size, and financial knowledge.

## How to Verify MAS Regulation

To check if a broker is genuinely MAS-regulated:

1. Visit the [MAS Financial Institutions Directory](https://eservices.mas.gov.sg/fid)
2. Search by broker name or CMS license number
3. Verify the license status is "Active"
4. Check the licensed activities include "Dealing in Capital Markets Products"

**Never trade with a broker that claims MAS regulation but can't provide a verifiable CMS license number.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to pay tax on forex trading profits in Singapore?

**Generally, no.** Singapore does not impose capital gains tax. If forex trading is your primary source of income (i.e., you're a full-time trader), the IRAS may classify your gains as taxable income. However, for most retail traders, forex profits are considered capital gains and are **tax-free**. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

**MAS limits retail leverage to 20:1 for major pairs and 10:1 for minor/exotic pairs.** This is significantly lower than what's available in other jurisdictions (e.g., 500:1 in Australia for professional clients). **Yes, forex trading is fully legal and regulated in Singapore.** The MAS oversees all forex brokers operating in the country. Singapore is one of the world's largest forex trading centers. Trading with an MAS-regulated broker ensures your funds are protected under Singapore law.
