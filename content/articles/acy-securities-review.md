### What This Means For You

- **ASIC (AFSL 403795):** One of the most respected financial regulators globally. Requires segregated client funds at authorized deposit-taking institutions (Tier 1 Australian banks), strict capital adequacy compliance, regular external audits, and negative balance protection. **However, ASIC does NOT mandate a formal investor compensation scheme** (unlike FCA's FSCS or CySEC's ICF).
- **VFSC (Vanuatu):** Significantly weaker regulatory oversight. International clients may be onboarded through this entity with fewer protections.
- **Segregated Funds:** Mandatory under ASIC — client funds held in segregated trust accounts at authorized Australian banks, separate from company operating capital.
- **Negative Balance Protection:** Yes — retail clients cannot lose more than their deposit.
- **Two-Factor Authentication:** Available for account security.
- **SSL Encryption:** 256-bit encryption on all transactions.
