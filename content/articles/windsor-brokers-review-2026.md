| British Virgin Islands | Windsor Global Markets Ltd | FSC (Financial Services Commission) | Active |

### What the Multi-Regulation Approach Means

**The good news:** Windsor Brokers has made the effort to obtain local licenses in each market it serves, rather than relying solely on a single offshore entity. This demonstrates a commitment to operating within legal frameworks and provides clients with jurisdiction-specific regulatory protections.

**The reality check:** None of these regulators are considered Tier-1 authorities. Let's break down what that means:

- **FSA Seychelles:** A legitimate financial regulator that supervises non-bank financial services in Seychelles. However, it's generally considered a Tier-3/offshore regulator with less stringent requirements than FCA, ASIC, or CySEC. Investor protection mechanisms are limited compared to Tier-1 jurisdictions.

- **Jordan Securities Commission (JSC):** The primary regulator for Jordan's capital markets. The JSC provides local oversight and aims to ensure market integrity within Jordan. It's a regional regulator with solid local standing but limited international recognition.

- **Capital Markets Authority (CMA) Kenya:** Responsible for regulating capital markets in Kenya. The CMA has been strengthening its regulatory framework in recent years as East African financial markets develop. It provides legitimate local oversight.

- **FSC British Virgin Islands:** An autonomous regulatory authority for financial services in the BVI. While providing a regulatory framework, the FSC BVI is typically associated with lighter-touch regulation.

### Risk Assessment: Medium

**Our rating: Medium Risk.** Windsor Brokers is not a scam — it's a genuinely regulated broker operating across multiple jurisdictions. However, the absence of any Tier-1 regulatory license (FCA, ASIC, CySEC, or equivalent) means:

- No access to Tier-1 investor compensation schemes (like FSCS in the UK or ICF in Cyprus)
- Potentially less stringent capital adequacy requirements
- Fewer mandatory client fund protection mechanisms
- Limited recourse options if disputes arise

**For whom this matters less:** Traders in Jordan, Kenya, or other markets where Windsor holds local licenses may find the local regulatory oversight adequate and even preferable to dealing with an offshore broker with no local presence.
