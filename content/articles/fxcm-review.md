## Regulation & Trust — Is FXCM Safe?

FXCM's regulatory history requires honest discussion:

### The 2017 Incident
In January 2017, the US Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) fined FXCM $7 million and barred it from the US market for engaging in fraudulent activities — specifically, FXCM's "no dealing desk" model secretly had a market-making affiliate taking the opposite side of client trades. This was a serious breach of trust.

### Post-2017 Rebuild
Under Jefferies Financial Group ownership (Leucadia National at the time), FXCM exited the US, settled all regulatory matters, and focused on rebuilding in other jurisdictions. As of 2026, the broker holds the following licenses:

| **FSCA** (Financial Sector Conduct Authority) | Licensed | South Africa |
| **FSC** (Financial Services Commission) | Licensed | Mauritius |

### What This Means For You

- **Negative balance protection** — Provided for retail clients across all entities
- **Segregated client funds** — Held at tier-1 banks separate from FXCM operating capital
- **FSCS protection** — UK clients covered up to £85,000
- **Jefferies backing** — Parent company Jefferies Financial Group (NYSE: JEF) provides substantial financial stability and oversight

Since 2017, FXCM has had no further regulatory actions. The company has been transparent about its past and has implemented significant compliance changes. Whether this rebuild is sufficient to earn your trust is a personal decision, but the current regulatory framework is solid.

## Trading Technology & Execution

FXCM operates its trading infrastructure through Equinix data centers in New York (NY4) and London (LD4), providing low-latency execution to clients worldwide. The broker uses a Straight-Through Processing (STP) execution model, meaning client orders are passed directly to liquidity providers without dealer intervention.

Average execution speed is approximately 30-50 milliseconds, with the majority of orders filled at the requested price. The broker publishes execution quality statistics on its website, providing transparency around fill rates, slippage patterns, and price improvement data.

FXCM's Trading Station platform includes a notable feature called "Market Scanner" — a real-time feed showing the best bid and ask prices across major banks and liquidity providers. This gives traders visibility into the actual institutional prices being quoted, rather than just the broker's own spread.
