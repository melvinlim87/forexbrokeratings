## ECN vs STP vs Market Maker: What's the Difference?

Understanding execution models is critical for choosing the right broker. Here's how the three main models compare:

### ECN (Electronic Communication Network)

**How it works:** Your orders are matched directly with other market participants (banks, institutions, other traders) through an electronic network. The broker acts as a facilitator, not a counterparty.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Dealing Desk** | ❌ No — orders go directly to the market |
| **Spread** | Variable, from 0.0 pips (raw interbank) |
| **Commission** | Yes — typically $3–$7 per lot per side |
| **Conflict of Interest** | ❌ None — broker profits from volume, not your losses |
| **Requotes** | ❌ No requotes (market execution) |
| **Slippage** | Can occur — positive or negative (market conditions) |
| **Best For** | Scalpers, EA traders, high-volume traders |

### STP (Straight-Through Processing)

**How it works:** Your orders are forwarded directly to liquidity providers without a dealing desk, but the broker may add a markup to the spread rather than charging a separate commission.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Dealing Desk** | ❌ No — orders sent to liquidity providers |
| **Spread** | Variable, typically 0.5–1.5 pips (includes markup) |
| **Commission** | Usually $0 (built into spread) |
| **Conflict of Interest** | ⚠️ Minimal — broker adds markup but doesn't trade against you |
| **Requotes** | Rare |
| **Best For** | Swing traders, medium-volume traders |

### Market Maker (Dealing Desk)

**How it works:** The broker acts as the counterparty to your trades — they take the other side. When you buy EUR/USD, your broker sells it to you from their own book.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Dealing Desk** | ✅ Yes — broker is your counterparty |
| **Spread** | Fixed or variable, typically 1.0–2.0 pips |
| **Commission** | Usually $0 |
| **Conflict of Interest** | ⚠️ Yes — broker profits when you lose |
| **Requotes** | Possible — especially during volatility |
| **Best For** | Beginners, casual traders, those who prefer fixed spreads |

### How to Tell if a Broker Is Really ECN

Not all "ECN" claims are legitimate. Here's how to verify:

1. **Check for Level II/Market Depth** — True ECN brokers show you the order book with visible bids and asks from multiple liquidity providers. If your platform doesn't offer market depth, it's probably not true ECN.

2. **Look for Commission-Based Pricing** — True ECN brokers charge a commission because they don't markup the spread. If there's no commission, the spread is marked up, and you're likely on an STP or market maker account.

3. **Verify No Requotes** — ECN execution means market execution — your order is filled at the best available price without requotes. If you experience requotes, it's not ECN.

4. **Check the Broker's Revenue Model** — Publicly traded brokers (IG, Interactive Brokers) disclose their revenue model in annual reports. If the broker profits from client losses (B-book model), it's not pure ECN.

5. **Test with a Small Account** — Open a demo or micro account and observe: do spreads drop to 0.0 during liquid hours? Is Level II data available? Are there requotes? These are practical verification steps.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an ECN broker?

An ECN (Electronic Communication Network) broker connects your trades directly to a pool of liquidity providers — banks, institutions, and other traders — without a dealing desk. This means your broker doesn't take the other side of your trade and has no conflict of interest. ECN brokers charge a commission and offer raw interbank spreads from 0.0 pips.

### How do I know if a broker is really ECN?

Look for these indicators: (1) Level II market depth showing multiple liquidity providers, (2) Commission-based pricing (not spread-only), (3) No requotes — market execution, (4) Spreads that drop to 0.0 during liquid hours, (5) Published execution statistics. If a broker offers "ECN" with no commission and no Level II data, it's likely not true ECN.

### What is the difference between ECN and STP?

ECN brokers match your orders in an electronic network where multiple participants compete to provide the best price. STP brokers route your orders to liquidity providers but may add a markup to the spread. ECN offers tighter spreads with a commission; STP offers convenience with a slightly higher spread but no separate commission.

### Are ECN brokers safer than market makers?

ECN brokers eliminate the conflict of interest inherent in market making (where the broker profits when you lose), but safety ultimately depends on regulation. A regulated market maker (like IG) may be safer than an unregulated ECN broker. The ideal is a regulated ECN broker — which is why all brokers on this list are regulated.

### What is the minimum deposit for ECN brokers?
