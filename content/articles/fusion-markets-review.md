### What This Means For You

- **ASIC regulation** is the gold standard in the Asia-Pacific region. The Australian Securities & Investments Commission requires brokers to maintain minimum capital adequacy, submit to regular audits, and adhere to strict client money handling rules.
- **Segregated client funds** are held in trust accounts at National Australia Bank (NAB), one of Australia's Big Four banks, completely separate from Fusion Markets' operating funds.
- **Negative balance protection** is provided to all retail clients, ensuring you can never lose more than your deposited amount.
- **VFSC regulation** provides offshore capabilities for international clients who may require higher leverage options not available under ASIC's retail framework.

Since launching, Fusion Markets has maintained a clean regulatory record with no enforcement actions, compliance breaches, or major customer complaints. The broker processes significant daily volume relative to its size, and its ASIC license (AFSL 226199) provides the same level of regulatory protection as any other Australian broker.

### Who Owns Fusion Markets?
