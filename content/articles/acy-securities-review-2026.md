### ASIC Regulation — Tier-1 Protection

ACY's ASIC authorization (AFSL 447917) provides:

- **Segregated client funds** — Per ASIC requirements, client money must be held in separate trust accounts
- **Capital adequacy** — ASIC requires brokers to maintain minimum net tangible assets
- **Regular audits** — Independent financial audits and compliance reviews
- **Australian Financial Services Compensation** — Access to compensation mechanisms where applicable
- **Corporations Act compliance** — Subject to the Australian Corporations Act 2001
- **Negative balance protection** — Standard for retail clients
- **Responsible lending obligations** — Must ensure products are suitable for clients

ASIC is widely regarded as one of the top 3 financial regulators globally (alongside the FCA and CySEC), making ACY's authorization a meaningful trust signal.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Is ACY Securities regulated?
Yes. ACY Securities Pty Ltd holds Australian Financial Services Licence (AFSL) 447917 from ASIC, one of the world's most respected financial regulators. International clients may be served through a Vanuatu entity regulated by the VFSC.

The minimum deposit is $50 for both the Standard and Raw ECN accounts. The Pro/Platinum account requires $20,000.

On the Standard account, EUR/USD spreads start from 0.8 pips. On the Raw ECN account, spreads start from 0.0 pips with a $6 round-turn commission per standard lot.

### What is ACY Securities' ECN commission?
ACY charges $6 round turn per standard lot on Raw ECN accounts. This is slightly less than IC Markets and Pepperstone, which charge $7 round turn.

ACY supports MetaTrader 4 (MT4), MetaTrader 5 (MT5), its proprietary AC Trader web platform, and a mobile app for iOS and Android.

Yes. ACY's pure ECN model fully supports scalping, hedging, and Expert Advisor (EA) trading. VPS hosting is compatible with MT4 and MT5.

### What payment methods does ACY Securities accept?
ACY accepts wire transfers, credit/debit cards, Skrill, Neteller, POLi (Australia), BPAY (Australia), cryptocurrency, and local bank transfers. All deposits and withdrawals are fee-free.
