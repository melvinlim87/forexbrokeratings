## Regulation & Safety

### Regulatory Licenses

| Entity | Regulator | License Number | Jurisdiction |
|--------|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| InstaTrade Corporation Ltd | **FSC BVI** | **#SIBA/L/14/1082** | British Virgin Islands |
| Instant Trading EU Ltd | **IFSC Belize** | **#000138/210** | Belize (offshore) |
| InstaForex Companies Group | **CySEC** | **#266/15** | Cyprus (EU entity) |

### What This Means

The majority of InstaForex's 7 million accounts are served through the **BVI entity** — a tier-3 offshore jurisdiction. The FSC BVI provides minimal regulatory oversight compared to the FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), or even CySEC (Cyprus).

The **CySEC entity (#266/15)** exists but serves primarily European clients. Most international traders, particularly in CIS and Asian markets, are routed through the offshore BVI or Belize entities.

### Client Fund Protection

- ⚠️ Segregated accounts claimed (but not independently verified under offshore entities)
- ❌ No investor compensation fund for BVI/Belize clients
- ✅ CySEC clients: Investor Compensation Fund up to €20,000
- ⚠️ Negative balance protection claimed
- ❌ Limited regulatory recourse for offshore clients

**Verdict on safety:** If you're an EU client served by the CySEC entity, your funds have reasonable protection. If you're an international client through the BVI or Belize entities, you're operating with minimal regulatory safety nets.

## FAQ

### Is InstaForex safe to trade with?
InstaForex has been operating since 2007 with over 7 million registered accounts. However, its primary regulatory entity is in the British Virgin Islands (FSC BVI, license #SIBA/L/14/1082) — a tier-3 offshore jurisdiction. EU clients served by the CySEC entity (#266/15) have stronger protection. International clients should exercise caution.

### What is the minimum deposit at InstaForex?
Just **$1** for micro/Cent accounts. Standard and Eurica accounts also start at $1. ECN accounts require $1,000. This is the lowest minimum deposit in the industry.

### Why are InstaForex's fixed spreads so wide?
InstaForex's Standard fixed spread accounts operate as market maker (dealing desk) accounts, where the broker must quote prices during all market conditions. The wide spreads (3–7 pips on EUR/USD) buffer the broker's risk. For tighter spreads, use the ECN account (0.0–1.2 pips) or consider a different broker entirely.
