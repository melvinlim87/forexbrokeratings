## What Is Copy Trading?

Copy trading is a form of **social trading** where your trading account automatically replicates the trades of another trader (the "signal provider" or "portfolio manager"). When they open a position, the same position is opened in your account, scaled to your allocated capital.

Key concepts:

- **Signal Provider / Lead Trader**: The experienced trader whose trades you copy
- **Copier / Follower**: You — the investor replicating trades
- **Copy Ratio**: How your position sizes scale relative to the provider's
- **Drawdown**: The peak-to-trough decline in the provider's account — a key risk metric
- **Slippage**: Difference between the provider's fill price and your copy price

## Copy Trading Risk Management: Protect Your Capital

Copy trading carries real risks. Follow these guidelines:

### Position Sizing Rules

| Account Size | Max per Provider | Max Providers | Total Copy Allocation |
|---|---|---|---|
| $1,000 | $300 (30%) | 3 | $900 (90%) |
| $5,000 | $1,000 (20%) | 5 | $4,000 (80%) |
| $10,000 | $2,000 (20%) | 5 | $8,000 (80%) |
| $50,000 | $7,500 (15%) | 8 | $40,000 (80%) |

### Red Flags to Watch For

- **Hockey stick returns**: Consistent 5%+ monthly returns with no losing months are suspicious
- **No drawdown history**: Every trader has drawdowns — if none are shown, data may be incomplete
- **Short track record**: Require at least 12 months of verified performance
- **Over-concentration**: Providers who only trade one pair or use martingale strategies
- **Sudden strategy changes**: If a provider's style changes overnight, unfollow immediately

### Best Practices

1. **Diversify across 3–5 providers** with different trading styles
2. **Set maximum drawdown limits** (most platforms allow this)
3. **Start with demo or small amounts** to test provider consistency
4. **Monitor weekly** — don't set and forget for months
5. **Keep 20% cash reserve** for margin buffer during drawdowns

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is copy trading profitable?

Copy trading **can be profitable** if you select the right signal providers and manage risk properly. Studies suggest that most retail traders lose money, so copying a consistently profitable trader gives you an edge. However, past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Diversify across multiple providers, set drawdown limits, and monitor regularly.

### What's the difference between copy trading and mirror trading?

**Copy trading** automatically replicates another trader's positions in your account in real time. **Mirror trading** uses algorithmic strategies (not individual traders) that you subscribe to. The terms are often used interchangeably, but copy trading specifically refers to following human traders, while mirror trading often follows automated systems.

### How much money do I need to start copy trading?

**You can start with as little as $50** (Vantage) or $100 (ZuluTrade via AAAFx). eToro requires $200 per copied trader. However, practical minimums depend on the signal provider's position sizes — if they trade standard lots and you only have $200, your account will be over-leveraged. A realistic starting capital is $500–$1,000.

### Do signal providers know I'm copying them?

**Usually not.** Most platforms aggregate copier data, so individual providers see total AUM (assets under management) copying them but not individual copier details. On eToro, providers can see the number of copiers and total AUM but not individual account information.

### Can I lose more than my initial investment?

**It depends on the broker and platform.** Regulated brokers in the EU, UK, and Australia are required to offer **negative balance protection**, meaning you can't lose more than your account balance. However, some offshore brokers and professional accounts may not offer this protection. Always verify before copying with leverage.

### Which copy trading platform is best for US residents?

**eToro is the primary option for US residents**, though US users can only copy crypto traders (not forex CFDs) due to CFTC regulations. MetaTrader Signals also work with US-regulated brokers like FOREX.com and OANDA. US forex copy trading options are limited due to regulatory restrictions.
