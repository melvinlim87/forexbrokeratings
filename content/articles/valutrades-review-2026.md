## Regulation & Safety: Dual Tier-1 Protection

This is where Valutrades genuinely shines. The broker holds licenses from two tier-1 regulators plus one offshore entity:

| Regulator | License Number | Entity | Jurisdiction |
|-----------|---------------|--------|--------------|
| **FCA (UK)** | **#580194** | Valutrades Ltd | United Kingdom |
| **CySEC (Cyprus)** | **#427/23** | Valutrades (Europe) Ltd | EU/EEA |
| **SCB (Bahamas)** | **SIA-F206** | Valutrades (Bahamas) Ltd | International |

### What This Means for Your Funds

- **UK clients:** Protected by the **Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS)** — up to **£85,000** per person if Valutrades fails.
- **EU/EEA clients:** Protected by the **Investor Compensation Fund (ICF)** under CySEC regulation.
- **All EEA clients:** Full **negative balance protection** — you can't lose more than your deposit.
- **Segregated funds:** Client money held in tier-1 banks, separate from company operating funds.
- **Bahamas clients:** Higher leverage (up to 1:500) but lighter regulatory protection.

### Why This Matters

Having **both FCA and CySEC** licenses is a significant differentiator. The FCA is widely considered the gold standard of financial regulation, requiring strict capital adequacy, regular auditing, and transparent reporting. The CySEC license provides EU passporting rights, allowing Valutrades to serve clients across the entire European Economic Area.

For comparison, many competitors in the low-deposit ECN space are regulated only in offshore jurisdictions (BVI, SVG, Belize). Valutrades' dual tier-1 status provides a level of trust and fund safety that offshore brokers simply cannot match.
