## Regulation & Safety

### Pepperstone Regulation

Pepperstone holds licenses from **six regulators** across multiple jurisdictions:

- **ASIC** (Australia) — #147055744 — Primary license
- **FCA** (UK) — #684312 — Critical for EU/UK clients
- **CySEC** (Cyprus) — #388/20 — European operations
- **DFSA** (Dubai) — Middle East operations
- **CMA** (Kenya) — African expansion
- **SCB** (Bahamas) — International/offshore clients

The **FCA license** is a significant differentiator. FCA-regulated clients benefit from the **FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme)**, which protects up to **£85,000** per client if Pepperstone UK becomes insolvent.

### IC Markets Regulation

IC Markets holds **three regulatory licenses**:

- **ASIC** (Australia) — #335692 — Primary license
- **CySEC** (Cyprus) — #362/18 — European operations
- **FSA Seychelles** — International/offshore clients

IC Markets is **not FCA-regulated**, meaning UK/EU clients use the CySEC entity. CySEC offers the **ICF (Investor Compensation Fund)**, which protects up to **€20,000** per client — less than the FCA's £85,000 FSCS protection.

**Winner: Pepperstone** — Six licenses vs three, and the FCA license provides significantly stronger investor protection for UK clients.

## Execution Quality & Speed

### Pepperstone Execution

- **Execution type**: STP (Straight-Through Processing)
- **Average speed**: 30ms
- **Server locations**: Equinix LD5 (London), NY4 (New York), TY3 (Tokyo)
- **No dealing desk** — orders routed directly to liquidity providers
- **Slippage**: Minimal; positive slippage reported on ~30% of orders during normal conditions

### IC Markets Execution

- **Execution type**: True ECN with 25+ liquidity providers
- **Average speed**: 36ms
- **Server locations**: Equinix NY4 (New York), LD5 (London)
- **Liquidity providers**: 25+ banks and dark pool venues
- **Slippage**: Low; positive slippage occurs during favorable market conditions

Both brokers provide **institutional-grade execution** with no requotes, no order manipulation, and no dealing desk intervention. Pepperstone is slightly faster on average (30ms vs 36ms), but IC Markets compensates with deeper liquidity pool access.

**Winner: Pepperstone** — Slightly faster average execution, though the difference is marginal in practice. Both are excellent.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which broker has tighter spreads: Pepperstone or IC Markets?

**IC Markets has tighter spreads** on both standard and raw accounts. On EUR/USD, IC Markets averages 0.8 pips (standard) and 0.0–0.1 pips (raw) vs Pepperstone's 1.0 pip (standard) and 0.0–0.3 pips (raw). The all-in cost difference is approximately $2 per standard lot on the raw account.

### Is Pepperstone or IC Markets better for EA trading?

**Both are excellent for EA trading** with zero restrictions on scalping, hedging, news trading, or algorithmic strategies. IC Markets has a slight edge due to deeper liquidity (25+ providers) and easier free VPS access. Pepperstone counters with Smart Trader Tools that enhance EA management.

### Which broker is safer?

**Pepperstone has stronger regulatory coverage** with six licenses including FCA (#684312), which provides £85,000 FSCS protection for UK clients. IC Markets is regulated by ASIC and CySEC, offering €20,000 ICF protection for EU clients. Both are safe, reputable brokers — but Pepperstone's FCA license provides the strongest investor protection.

### Can I use both Pepperstone and IC Markets?

**Yes, and many professional traders do.** Some run EAs on IC Markets for tighter spreads while using Pepperstone's TradingView integration for discretionary trading. There's no restriction on having accounts at both brokers.

### Which broker is better for scalping?

**Both are excellent for scalping.** Neither broker imposes minimum holding times, requotes, or order manipulation. IC Markets' tighter raw spreads give scalpers a cost advantage. Pepperstone's slightly faster execution (30ms vs 36ms) benefits latency-sensitive strategies. The practical difference is minimal.

### Does IC Markets offer TradingView integration?

**No.** As of 2026, IC Markets does not offer direct TradingView integration. You can use TradingView for analysis and then execute trades through MT4/MT5/cTrader, but you cannot connect your IC Markets account to TradingView for one-click trading. Pepperstone does offer this integration.
