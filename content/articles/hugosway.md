### What SVG "Regulation" Actually Means

This is critically important to understand:

- **SVG FSA does not regulate forex brokers.** The SVG FSA has explicitly stated that it does not license, regulate, or supervise forex trading or CFD brokers.
- **No capital adequacy requirements.** Unlike FCA, CySEC, or ASIC-regulated brokers, Hugo's Way is not required to maintain minimum capital reserves.
- **No investor compensation scheme.** If Hugo's Way were to become insolvent, there is no FSCS (UK), ICF (Cyprus), or equivalent compensation fund to protect client deposits.
- **No formal dispute resolution.** There is no financial ombudsman service or regulatory body to escalate complaints to.
- **No negative balance protection requirement.** While Hugo's Way claims to offer negative balance protection, this is a self-imposed policy rather than a regulatory requirement — meaning it could be changed at any time.

### How Hugo's Way Mitigates Risk

To its credit, Hugo's Way implements several measures voluntarily:

- **Segregated client funds** — Client money is held separately from company operating funds
- **Tier-1 liquidity partnerships** — Trades are executed through institutional-grade liquidity providers
- **No dealing desk** — ECN/STP model eliminates the broker taking the opposite side of client trades
- **Transparent fee structure** — No hidden charges or complex fee schedules

However, these are **self-imposed policies**, not regulatory requirements. They offer some reassurance but are not equivalent to the protections provided by FCA, CySEC, or ASIC regulation.
