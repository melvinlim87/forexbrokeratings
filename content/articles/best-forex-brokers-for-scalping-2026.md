## What Is Forex Scalping?

Forex scalping is a trading strategy that aims to profit from small price movements by executing a high volume of trades over very short time frames — often seconds to minutes. Scalpers might enter and exit positions dozens or even hundreds of times per day, capturing 2-10 pips per trade.

Because scalping relies on razor-thin margins, the broker you choose matters more for scalping than for almost any other trading style. A few tenths of a pip in extra spread, a few milliseconds of slower execution, or a "last look" delay from your broker can turn a profitable scalping strategy into a losing one.

### What Makes a Broker Good for Scalping?

Not all brokers welcome scalpers. Some impose minimum holding times, delay execution, or widen spreads during volatile conditions. The best brokers for scalping share these characteristics:

- **Tight raw spreads** — Sub-pip spreads on major pairs, ideally from 0.0 pips
- **Low commissions** — Competitive per-lot commissions ($3.50-$7.00 round turn)
- **Fast execution** — Sub-100ms order execution with no requotes
- **No scalping restrictions** — No minimum holding time, no penalty for quick trades
- **NDD/ECN execution** — No Dealing Desk or ECN model to avoid broker conflict of interest
- **High liquidity access** — Deep liquidity pools for minimal slippage
- **Stable platforms** — Reliable MT4/MT5/cTrader with one-click trading

We've evaluated dozens of brokers against these criteria and selected the top 5 for 2026.

## Scalping Tips for Beginners

If you're new to scalping, here are essential tips to get started:

### 1. Start with a Demo Account
Every broker on this list offers free demo accounts. Practice your scalping strategy on a demo for at least 1-2 months before trading real money. Focus on execution speed, spread costs, and emotional control.

### 2. Use One-Click Trading
Scalping requires speed. Enable one-click trading on your platform to eliminate the confirmation dialog that costs precious seconds.

### 3. Focus on Major Pairs
EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and AUD/USD offer the tightest spreads and deepest liquidity. Avoid exotic pairs — wider spreads eat into your scalping profits.

### 4. Manage Your Risk Per Trade
Never risk more than 0.5-1% of your account on a single scalp. With 50+ trades per day, a few bad trades can compound quickly if position sizes are too large.

### 5. Monitor Your All-In Costs
Track your total trading costs (spread + commission) per trade. If your average profit per trade is 3 pips but your all-in cost is 1.5 pips, you're giving up 50% of your edge to the broker.

### 6. Use a VPS
If you're running Expert Advisors or automated scalping strategies, a Virtual Private Server (VPS) ensures your platform stays connected 24/7 with minimal latency to your broker's servers.

### 7. Avoid Trading During Major News Events
Spreads widen dramatically during high-impact news releases (NFP, FOMC, ECB). Unless you're specifically trading the news, avoid opening new scalps in the minutes surrounding major announcements.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Is scalping legal?
Yes, scalping is a completely legal trading strategy in all major jurisdictions. However, not all brokers allow it — some impose minimum holding times or penalize rapid trading. All five brokers in this guide explicitly allow scalping.

### 2. What is the best time frame for scalping?
Most scalpers use 1-minute (M1) or 5-minute (M5) charts. Some also reference the 15-minute (M15) chart for trend context. The key is finding a time frame that gives you enough price movement to capture 2-10 pips per trade.

### 3. How much money do I need to start scalping?
While brokers like Fusion Markets and Pepperstone have no minimum deposit, a practical starting capital for scalping is $500-$1,000. This allows you to trade micro-lots (0.01) while keeping risk per trade at 1-2% of your account.

### 4. Can I scalp with a Standard (no-commission) account?
Technically yes, but it's not recommended. Standard accounts have wider spreads (typically 1.0+ pips on EUR/USD), which eat significantly into scalping profits. Raw/ECN accounts with tight spreads + commission are always better for scalping.

### 5. Which broker has the fastest execution for scalping?
Pepperstone averages ~30ms execution, making it the fastest on our list. However, IC Markets (~35ms) and FP Markets (~40ms) are extremely close. All three are fast enough for professional scalping.

### 6. Do I need a VPS for scalping?
Not necessarily, but it helps — especially if you're running automated strategies. A VPS ensures your platform stays connected with consistent low latency, even if your home internet drops. IC Markets, Pepperstone, and FP Markets offer free VPS for qualifying accounts.

### 7. Can I scalp crypto with these brokers?
Yes, particularly Eightcap, which offers 86+ crypto CFD pairs with competitive spreads. IC Markets and FP Markets also offer crypto CFDs, though with fewer pairs. Keep in mind that crypto spreads are generally wider than forex spreads, so crypto scalping requires larger price movements.
