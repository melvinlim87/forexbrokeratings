Founded in 2013, this broker offers raw spreads from **0.0 pips**, commissions as low as **$3 per lot per side**, and leverage up to **1:1000** — all with a **$10 minimum deposit**.

But Land-FX is regulated only in **Mauritius** and **St. Vincent & the Grenadines** — not the FCA, ASIC, or CySEC. In this **Land-FX review 2026**, we analyze whether the competitive trading conditions justify the offshore regulatory risk.

## Regulation & Safety

### Regulatory Licenses

| Jurisdiction | Regulator | License Number | Status |
|-------------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Mauritius | **Financial Services Commission (FSC)** | **GB24203734** | Active |
| St. Vincent & the Grenadines | **SVG IBC Registry** | **23627 IBC 2016** | Active |

### What This Means

Land Prime Ltd holds a **Global Business and Investment Dealer license** from the Mauritius FSC (GB24203734), which requires capital adequacy standards, client fund segregation, and regular reporting. However, Mauritius is classified as a **tier-3 jurisdiction** — meaning protections are significantly lighter than those under the FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), or CySEC (Cyprus).

The SVG IBC registration (23627 IBC 2016) is a business incorporation, not a financial services license. St. Vincent & the Grenadines does not have a comprehensive forex regulatory framework.

### Client Fund Protection

- ✅ Segregated client accounts (claimed)
- ⚠️ No independent verification of segregation practices
- ❌ No investor compensation fund (FSCS, ICF, etc.)
- ⚠️ Negative balance protection claimed (not guaranteed under offshore regulation)
- ❌ No Tier 1 regulatory oversight

**Key consideration:** The absence of tier-1 regulation means no access to compensation schemes like the UK's FSCS (up to £85,000) or Cyprus's ICF (up to €20,000). If Land-FX faces insolvency, clients have limited recourse.
