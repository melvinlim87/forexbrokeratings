## Understanding Forex Spreads: What Actually Matters

### What Is a Spread?

The **spread** is the difference between the bid (sell) price and the ask (buy) price of a currency pair. It's essentially the cost of entering a trade. A broker quoting EUR/USD at 1.0850/1.0852 has a 2-pip spread.

### Raw Spreads vs. Standard Spreads

| Feature | Standard Account | Raw/ECN Account |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Spread** | Marked up (1.0–1.5 pips EUR/USD) | Interbank (0.0–0.3 pips EUR/USD) |
| **Commission** | $0 | $3–$7 per lot per side |
| **All-in Cost** | ~1.2 pips | ~0.6–0.9 pips |
| **Best For** | Beginners, low volume | Scalpers, high volume, EA traders |

### All-In Cost: The Only Metric That Matters

Never compare spreads alone. Always calculate the **all-in cost**:

**All-in Cost = Spread + Commission (converted to pips)**

For example:
- **Broker A:** 0.2 pip spread + $3.50/lot commission = ~0.9 pip all-in cost
- **Broker B:** 0.0 pip spread + $7.00/lot commission = ~0.7 pip all-in cost
- **Broker C:** 1.0 pip spread + $0 commission = 1.0 pip all-in cost

Broker B is cheapest despite having the highest commission, because the zero spread more than compensates.

## FAQ

### What is a good spread for forex trading?

A good spread for EUR/USD is below 1.0 pip on a standard account or below 0.3 pips on a raw/ECN account. The industry average for raw accounts is approximately 0.2–0.5 pips for EUR/USD, plus a $3–$7 commission per lot. An all-in cost below 1.0 pip is considered competitive; below 0.7 pips is excellent.

### Are low spread brokers safe?

Low spreads don't determine a broker's safety — regulation does. All brokers in this guide hold at least one Tier 1 or Tier 2 regulatory licence (ASIC, FCA, CySEC, FMA). IC Markets (ASIC, CySEC), Pepperstone (FCA, ASIC, BaFin), and Tickmill (FCA, ASIC, CySEC) are among the most heavily regulated low-spread brokers available.

### What's the difference between a raw account and a standard account?

A raw account offers interbank spreads (from 0.0 pips) but charges a commission ($3–$7 per lot per side). A standard account has no commission but includes a spread markup (typically 0.8–1.5 pips). For active traders, raw accounts are almost always cheaper overall.

### Can I get 0 pip spreads?

Yes. Several brokers offer 0.0 pip spreads on EUR/USD during liquid market hours, including IC Markets, FP Markets, Tickmill, and BlackBull Markets. However, these are variable spreads — they can widen during news events or low-liquidity periods. The advertised 0.0 pips is the minimum, not a guaranteed fixed spread.

### Which broker has the lowest commission?

Fusion Markets has the lowest commission at $2.25 per lot per side ($4.50 round turn). Tickmill's Raw account is next at $3.00 per side ($6.00 RT). IC Markets and Pepperstone both charge $3.50 per side ($7.00 RT). Always factor in the spread alongside commission when comparing all-in costs.

### Do low spread brokers charge hidden fees?

The brokers in this guide are transparent about their fee structures. However, always check for: inactivity fees (Tickmill charges $15/month after 12 months), withdrawal fees (some charge for bank wires), and swap/overnight fees (standard across the industry). None of the brokers listed here charge hidden spread markups on their raw accounts.
