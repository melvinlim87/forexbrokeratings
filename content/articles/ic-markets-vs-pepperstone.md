- **CySEC** — License 388/20 (EU/EEA)
- **DFSA** — F004356 (Dubai)
- **SCB** — SIA-F217 (Bahamas)
- **BaFin** — Registered (Germany)
- **CMA** — Registered (Kenya)

### Verdict: Regulation

**🏆 Pepperstone wins.** Seven regulatory licenses vs. four. The FCA license (FRN 684312) is the critical differentiator — it provides FSCS protection up to £85,000 for UK traders and is widely regarded as the gold standard of financial regulation. IC Markets' lack of an FCA license means UK traders are onboarded through the ASIC or SCB entity, which, while still well-regulated, doesn't offer the same level of UK-specific protection.

Both brokers hold ASIC licenses, which provide strong regulatory oversight including segregated client funds and negative balance protection. Neither has had any major regulatory actions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is IC Markets better than Pepperstone?

It depends on your priorities. **IC Markets** is better for the tightest spreads (0.02 pips avg EUR/USD), deepest liquidity ($29B+ daily), and widest instrument range (2,250+). **Pepperstone** is better for FCA regulation, TradingView integration, $0 minimum deposit, and broader platform choice. Both charge $3.50/lot per side on their ECN accounts.

### Which has tighter spreads — IC Markets or Pepperstone?

**IC Markets** has tighter average spreads. On EUR/USD, IC Markets averages approximately 0.02 pips vs. Pepperstone's 0.1-0.3 pips. Both offer spreads from 0.0 pips as the minimum, but IC Markets' larger liquidity pool ($29B+ vs. $12B+) results in consistently tighter average pricing.

### Is Pepperstone safer than IC Markets?

**Pepperstone** has broader regulatory coverage with 7 licenses including FCA (FRN 684312), which provides FSCS protection up to £85,000 for UK clients. IC Markets has 4 licenses and no FCA. Both hold ASIC licenses (Pepperstone AFSL 414530, IC Markets AFSL 335692), which are equally strong. For UK-based traders, Pepperstone is safer due to FCA regulation. For Australian traders, both are equally safe under ASIC.

### Which is better for scalping — IC Markets or Pepperstone?

Both are excellent for scalping, but **IC Markets** has a slight edge due to deeper liquidity (25+ tier-1 providers) and tighter average spreads (0.02 pips). Deeper liquidity means less slippage during fast market moves, which matters most for scalpers executing rapid trades.

### Which is better for beginners — IC Markets or Pepperstone?

**Pepperstone** is better for beginners due to its $0 minimum deposit, TradingView integration (which is more intuitive than MT4), and slightly better customer support. IC Markets' $200 minimum deposit is a higher barrier to entry.

### Can I use both IC Markets and Pepperstone?

Yes. Many traders maintain accounts with both brokers. Some use IC Markets for high-frequency strategies (tighter spreads) and Pepperstone for swing trading with TradingView charts. Both brokers allow multiple accounts per person.

### Which has better platforms — IC Markets or Pepperstone?

**Pepperstone** offers more platform options (MT4, MT5, cTrader, and TradingView). IC Markets offers MT4, MT5, and cTrader. If you use or want TradingView, Pepperstone is the only choice. For cTrader specifically, both offer identical cTrader experiences.
