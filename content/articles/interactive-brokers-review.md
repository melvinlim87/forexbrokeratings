## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. Is Interactive Brokers safe and legitimate?
Absolutely. Interactive Brokers is regulated by the SEC, FINRA, FCA, CBI, ASIC, and 10+ other regulators globally. It is publicly listed on NASDAQ (IBKR), has operated since 1978, and offers SIPC protection up to $500,000 for US accounts (with excess coverage up to $30 million). It is one of the safest brokers in the world.

### 2. Is Interactive Brokers good for beginners?
Not primarily. While IBKR has simplified its interface over the years, TWS and the overall platform remain complex. IBKR Lite and the web platform are more accessible, but beginners may find better learning experiences at platforms like eToro, XM, or Fidelity. That said, if you're committed to learning, IBKR's tools reward the effort.

### 3. What is the minimum deposit at Interactive Brokers?
There is no minimum deposit requirement to open or maintain an IBKR account. You can start with any amount, though practical considerations (margin requirements, diversification) mean most traders start with at least $2,000-$10,000.

### 4. How does Interactive Brokers compare to Charles Schwab or Fidelity?
IBKR excels in global market access, options trading, API capabilities, and commission pricing. Schwab and Fidelity are better for casual US stock investors who value branch access, extensive research, and simplified interfaces. IBKR wins for active/global traders; Schwab/Fidelity win for set-it-and-forget-it US investors.

### 5. Does Interactive Brokers offer crypto trading?
Yes, but limited. In the US, crypto trading is facilitated through Paxos Trust Company. IBKR offers Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, and Bitcoin Cash trading. The selection is much smaller than dedicated crypto exchanges but is convenient for investors who want crypto exposure within a traditional brokerage account.
