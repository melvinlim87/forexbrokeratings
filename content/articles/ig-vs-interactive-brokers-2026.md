# IG vs Interactive Brokers 2026: Complete Comparison

The **IG vs Interactive Brokers** debate is one of the most common in online trading. Both are publicly listed, globally regulated, multi-asset brokers with decades of history. But they serve fundamentally different types of traders, and choosing between them can significantly impact your trading experience, costs, and access to markets.

IG (LSE: IGG) is the world's most regulated retail CFD broker, excelling in forex and leveraged derivatives with a polished proprietary platform. Interactive Brokers (NASDAQ: IBKR) is the world's largest online brokerage by market cap (~$70 billion), offering direct market access to 150+ exchanges with professional-grade tools.

In this comprehensive comparison, we break down every dimension that matters: fees, platforms, regulation, markets, execution, and suitability for different trader profiles.

> ⚠️ **Risk Warning:** CFDs are complex instruments. 69% of retail CFD accounts lose money with IG; 74–89% lose money with Interactive Brokers CFDs. This comparison is for informational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice.

## FAQ

### Is IG or Interactive Brokers better for forex trading?

Interactive Brokers offers lower forex trading costs (EUR/USD all-in cost of ~0.2–0.4 pips vs IG's ~0.6–1.0 pips). However, IG provides a more intuitive platform, guaranteed stop-loss orders, and wider CFD selection. For pure cost optimization, choose Interactive Brokers. For a better overall forex trading experience, choose IG.

### Can I trade real stocks on IG?

In most regions, IG offers CFDs on stocks — not actual share ownership. However, IG does offer direct share dealing in some markets (UK, Australia, and select European exchanges). Interactive Brokers offers actual share ownership on 150+ global exchanges, including fractional shares. If owning real stocks matters to you, Interactive Brokers is the better choice.

### Is Interactive Brokers safe?

Yes. Interactive Brokers is one of the safest brokers in the world. It's publicly traded on NASDAQ (IBKR) with a ~$70 billion market cap, regulated by the SEC, CFTC, FCA, ASIC, MAS, and IIROC, and offers SIPC protection up to $500,000 (plus Lloyd's excess coverage up to $30 million). It has been in operation since 1978 with no major security breaches or regulatory failures.

### Which broker is better for beginners: IG or Interactive Brokers?

IG is significantly better for beginners. The IG platform is more intuitive, IG Academy offers structured educational courses, customer support is more responsive, and the overall user experience is designed for retail traders. Interactive Brokers' TWS platform has a steep learning curve and is designed for experienced traders.

### Do both brokers offer demo accounts?

Yes. IG offers a demo account with $10,000 in virtual funds that can be used for 30 days (renewable). Interactive Brokers offers a paper trading account with virtual funds. Both are free and allow you to practice trading without risking real money.

### Which broker is better for Singapore traders?

IG is better for Singapore traders. IG holds a direct MAS CMS licence (No. 100461) and has a physical office in Singapore. Interactive Brokers is also MAS-licensed, but IG's proprietary platform is more user-friendly, its SGD spreads are competitive, and its customer support for Singapore clients is stronger.
