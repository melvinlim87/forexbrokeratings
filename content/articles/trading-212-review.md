# Trading 212 Review 2026: Is Commission-Free Trading Really Worth It?

Trading 212 has exploded in popularity over the past few years, amassing roughly **4.5 million funded accounts** and becoming one of Europe's most-downloaded trading apps. Founded in 2004 in Sofia, Bulgaria, the broker pivoted from traditional forex to commission-free stock trading in 2017 — a move that turned it into a retail investing juggernaut.

But popularity doesn't always equal quality. In this Trading 212 review, we break down the broker's regulation, trading conditions, platform, fees, and everything in between to help you decide if it deserves your money.

## Regulation & Safety: Is Trading 212 Legit?

### Multi-Tier Regulatory Coverage

Trading 212 holds some of the strongest regulatory credentials in the retail brokerage space:

| Regulator | Tier | Entity | License Number |
|-----------|------|--------|----------------|
| **FCA (UK)** | Tier 1 | Trading 212 UK Limited | #609146 |
| **CySEC (Cyprus)** | Tier 1 | Trading 212 Markets Ltd | #398/21 |
| **BaFin (Germany)** | Tier 1 | FXFlat Bank GmbH (acquired 2024) | — |
| **ASIC (Australia)** | Tier 1 | Trading 212 Australia Pty Ltd | — |
| **FSC (Bulgaria)** | Tier 2 | Trading 212 Ltd | — |

Having **three or more Tier-1 licenses** puts Trading 212 in the upper echelon of retail brokers. UK clients benefit from FSCS protection (up to £85,000), while EU clients are covered by the Investor Compensation Fund (ICF) for up to €20,000. Additionally, Trading 212 carries a **€1 million Lloyd's of London indemnity insurance policy** — a feature most competitors don't offer.

### Fund Safety

- ✅ Segregated client funds at Tier-1 banks
- ✅ Negative balance protection (EU/UK retail clients)
- ✅ FSCS coverage (UK) + ICF coverage (EU)
- ✅ Additional €1M Lloyd's insurance

### Notable Concern: The GameStop Incident

In January 2021, Trading 212 temporarily restricted buy orders on GameStop and other meme stocks during the short squeeze. While the broker cited liquidity provider constraints, the move damaged trust among some users. It's worth noting that many brokers imposed similar restrictions at the time.

## In-Depth Platform Analysis

### Web Platform Experience

Trading 212's web platform is designed with simplicity at its core. The dashboard provides a clean overview of your portfolio, watchlist, and open positions without overwhelming you with data. Charting is functional but basic — you get line, bar, and candlestick charts with a handful of technical indicators (moving averages, Bollinger Bands, RSI, MACD). For comparison, MetaTrader 5 offers 80+ built-in indicators and 21 timeframes, while Trading 212's platform maxes out at a fraction of that.

Order types are limited to market, limit, and stop orders. There are no trailing stops, OCO (one-cancels-other) orders, or conditional order types that advanced traders rely on. The one-click trading feature works smoothly, and the order ticket is intuitive — but power users will quickly feel constrained.

### Pies & AutoInvest Feature

One of Trading 212's unique features is **Pies** — a portfolio-building tool that lets you create diversified investment allocations. You select stocks and ETFs, assign percentage weights, and the platform automatically invests according to your allocation. You can also browse and copy community-created Pies, making it a lightweight form of social investing.

AutoInvest takes this further by automatically investing recurring deposits into your chosen Pie. It's an excellent feature for dollar-cost averaging and passive investors who want a "set and forget" approach. However, Pies only work with the Invest account (real stocks/ETFs), not CFDs.

### Mobile App Deep Dive

The Trading 212 mobile app is where the broker truly shines. Rated **4.4/5 on Google Play** with over 10 million downloads, it's one of the most polished trading apps available. The app mirrors most of the web platform's functionality, with smooth navigation, quick order execution, and push notifications for price alerts.

However, the mobile app has notable gaps: there's no news feed, limited charting tools, and no ability to set complex order types. The Pies feature works well on mobile, and the overall UX is genuinely best-in-class for beginners.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Trading 212 really free?

Yes, for stock and ETF investing. There are no commissions, custody fees, or platform fees. Trading 212 earns revenue through currency conversion fees (~0.15%), CFD spreads, and stock lending. However, CFD trading is expensive due to wide spreads.

### Does Trading 212 offer MetaTrader?

No. Trading 212 exclusively supports its proprietary platform on web and mobile. There is no MT4, MT5, TradingView, or API access.

### Is Trading 212 safe and regulated?

Yes. Trading 212 is regulated by the FCA (license #609146), CySEC (#398/21), BaFin, and ASIC. Client funds are segregated, and UK clients have FSCS protection up to £85,000. The broker also carries €1M in Lloyd's indemnity insurance.

## Our Verdict

Trading 212 is an **excellent platform for beginners and passive investors** who want commission-free stock and ETF trading with strong regulatory protection. The €1 minimum deposit, Pies feature, and intuitive app make it one of the most accessible brokers in Europe.

However, **active forex traders should look elsewhere**. The 2.7-pip EUR/USD spread is roughly triple the industry average, and the lack of MT4/MT5, API access, or algorithmic trading support means serious traders will feel constrained.

**Our Rating: 3.8/5** — Outstanding for stock investing, poor for active CFD trading.

If you primarily trade stocks and ETFs passively, Trading 212 is hard to beat. If you trade forex actively, consider brokers like IC Markets, Pepperstone, or FP Markets for significantly tighter spreads and better platform support.
