# NAGA Review 2026: The Social Trading Broker Backed by a Public Company


---

## Overview

NAGA is a social trading broker that has carved out a unique position in the crowded CFD industry. Founded in 2015 by Benjamin Bilski, NAGA's parent company — The NAGA Group AG — is publicly listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange (WKN: A41YCM | ISIN: DE000A41YCM0). This public listing provides a level of financial transparency that is rare among forex and CFD brokers.

Headquartered in Limassol, Cyprus, with a global operational base in Seychelles, NAGA offers over 4,000 trading instruments spanning forex, stocks, cryptocurrencies, commodities, indices, ETFs, and bonds. But what truly sets NAGA apart is its Autocopy technology — a one-click copy trading system that lets users automatically replicate the trades of experienced investors from a community of over 2 million users.

NAGA operates through two regulated entities: NAGA Markets Europe Ltd, regulated by CySEC (License 204/13) for EU clients, and NAGA Capital Ltd, regulated by the FSA of Seychelles (License SD026) for international clients.

In this comprehensive 2026 review, we examine NAGA's regulation, trading conditions, social trading features, platforms, fees, and whether this publicly listed broker lives up to its ambitious promise.

**Quick Verdict:** NAGA is an excellent choice for beginners and social traders who want to follow experienced traders via Autocopy. The Frankfurt listing adds credibility, and the 4,000+ instrument range is impressive. However, the $250 minimum deposit is higher than many competitors, and non-EU clients are served by the less-regulated Seychelles entity.

### Corporate Transparency

NAGA's parent company, The NAGA Group AG, is listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange. This means:

- **Public financial reporting** — Quarterly and annual filings available to anyone
- **Board oversight** — Subject to German Securities Exchange Act regulations
- **Shareholder accountability** — Management answers to public shareholders
- **Audited financials** — Annual audits by independent firms

This level of transparency is uncommon in the forex industry and adds a layer of trust that private brokers simply cannot match.

### What This Means For You

**For EU clients (CySEC entity):**
- ✅ Tier-1 regulation under MiFID II
- ✅ Investor Compensation Fund (ICF) — up to €20,000 protection
- ✅ Negative balance protection
- ✅ Segregated client funds
- ✅ Access to EU cross-border dispute resolution

**For non-EU clients (FSA Seychelles entity):**
- ⚠️ Moderate offshore regulation
- ✅ Negative balance protection
- ✅ Segregated client funds
- ❌ No investor compensation scheme
- ⚠️ Higher leverage available (up to 1:1000) but with fewer protections

**Risk Level: MEDIUM**

The CySEC entity is well-regulated, but the majority of NAGA's global clients are served through the Seychelles entity, which offers fewer protections. The public listing of the parent company partially offsets this concern through financial transparency.

## Social Trading — NAGA's Killer Feature

NAGA's Autocopy system is the core differentiator and the primary reason most traders choose this platform.

### How Autocopy Works

1. **Browse the Leaderboard** — View ranked traders by performance, risk score, win rate, and follower count
2. **Select a Trader** — Choose who to copy based on transparent performance metrics
3. **Set Your Parameters** — Define copy amount per trade, maximum daily exposure, and stop-loss limits
4. **Autocopy** — Every trade the lead trader makes is automatically replicated in your account
5. **Monitor & Adjust** — Track performance in real-time and stop copying anytime

### Community Features

- **Social Feed** — Real-time trading discussions and market commentary
- **Leaderboard** — Gamified ranking of top-performing traders
- **Trading Signals** — Community-generated trade ideas
- **NAGA Messenger** — Built-in communication between traders

### Social Trading Risks

Copy trading is not a guaranteed profit strategy. Key risks include:

- Past performance doesn't guarantee future results
- Lead traders can have significant drawdowns
- Slippage between lead trader's execution and yours
- Over-concentration if copying a single trader
- Emotional pressure to chase top leaderboard performers
