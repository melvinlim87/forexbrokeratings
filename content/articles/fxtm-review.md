## Executive Summary

FXTM (ForexTime) has established itself as one of the most recognisable names in online forex and CFD trading since its founding in 2011 by Andrey Dashin, the entrepreneur behind Alpari. Headquartered in Limassol, Cyprus, FXTM operates across multiple continents with a strong presence in Africa, the Middle East, and Asia, serving over 2 million registered clients worldwide.

In this honest 2026 review, we break down regulation, trading conditions, fees, platforms, pros, cons, and who FXTM is actually best for — cutting through the marketing to give you the real picture.

**Quick Verdict:** FXTM is a well-rounded, well-regulated broker that excels in copy trading and educational resources. It's a particularly strong choice for traders in emerging markets (Africa, Middle East, Southeast Asia) where FXTM has invested heavily in local presence. However, its spread-only pricing on most accounts and limited CFD range compared to larger competitors hold it back from the top tier.

| **FCA** entity (Exinity UK Ltd) | FRN 777911 | UK |
| **IFSC** (International Financial Services Commission) | IFSC/60/353/TS/17 | Belize |

### What This Means For You

- **Tier-1 regulation** — The FCA (UK) and CySEC (Cyprus) provide the highest level of regulatory protection available in Europe and the UK.
- **Negative balance protection** — You cannot lose more than your account balance under FCA and CySEC entities.
- **Segregated client funds** — Held in separate accounts at tier-1 banks, independent from FXTM's operating capital.
- **FSCS protection** — Up to £85,000 for UK FCA-regulated accounts.
- **ICF protection** — Up to €20,000 for EU/CySEC-regulated accounts.
- **Multiple regional entities** — Depending on where you sign up, you may fall under different regulatory regimes with varying levels of protection. Check your client agreement to confirm which entity you're under.

**Important:** FXTM restructured its entity structure in 2022. UK clients are now served by Exinity UK Ltd (FRN 777911). Make sure you understand which entity your account falls under, as regulatory protections vary significantly between FCA/CySEC and offshore entities like IFSC Belize.

## Pros & Cons

### ✅ Pros

1. **Strong multi-jurisdiction regulation** — FCA (UK), CySEC (Cyprus), and FSCA (South Africa) provide tier-1 regulatory coverage across three major markets. You're well-protected under these entities.

2. **Excellent educational resources** — FXTM invests heavily in trader education with free webinars, video courses, e-books, a glossary, and a forex trading academy. This is one of the best educational programmes in the industry.

3. **Copy trading (FXTM Invest)** — Built-in copy trading platform allows beginners to replicate the strategies of experienced traders. Performance tracking is transparent and easy to understand.

4. **Cent account for beginners** — The $10 cent account lets new traders learn with real money at extremely low risk. This is genuinely valuable for education.

5. **Competitive ECN Zero pricing** — At $4/lot round-turn, the ECN commission is lower than most competitors, and spreads starting from 0.0 pips make the all-in cost very competitive.

6. **Strong regional presence** — FXTM has localised offices and support in 20+ countries, with particular strength in Africa (Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa), the Middle East, and Southeast Asia. Localised deposit methods and language support.

7. **Low inactivity fee** — $5/month after 6 months is significantly lower than the £12-15/month charged by many competitors.

### ❌ Cons

1. **Average Standard account spreads** — EUR/USD at 1.5 pips on the Standard account is nothing special. Many competitors offer sub-1.0 pip spreads on their standard accounts.

2. **Limited instrument range** — 250+ instruments is respectable but thin compared to the thousands offered by City Index (13,500+) or IG (17,000+).

3. **Limited cryptocurrency offering** — Only 5+ crypto CFDs versus 10-30+ offered by competitors. FXTM has been conservative with its crypto expansion.

4. **Restructured entity structure** — The 2022 restructuring into separate entities (including Exinity UK) may confuse some clients about which regulator protects them.

5. **Currency conversion fee** — The 1.5% markup on currency conversions can add up if you're depositing/withdrawing in a currency different from your account base currency.

6. **No proprietary desktop platform** — FXTM relies entirely on MetaTrader. While MT4/MT5 are excellent, some traders prefer proprietary platforms with integrated social features.

7. **Higher minimum deposit for ECN** — The $200 ECN Zero minimum is reasonable but higher than the $0 minimums offered by some competitors.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is FXTM a legitimate and safe broker?
Yes. FXTM is regulated by the FCA (FRN 777911), CySEC (License 185/12), and FSCA (FSP 46614) — three of the most respected financial regulators in the world. Client funds are segregated, negative balance protection is provided, and UK clients have access to FSCS protection up to £85,000.

### 2. What is the minimum deposit for FXTM?
The minimum deposit varies by account type: $10 for the Cent Account, $100 for the Standard and Shares accounts, $200 for the ECN Zero Account, and $25,000 for the FXTM Advantage Account. The $10 cent account makes FXTM accessible to almost anyone.

### 3. Does FXTM have copy trading?
Yes. FXTM Invest is FXTM's built-in copy trading platform. It allows you to browse and follow experienced traders, viewing their performance history, risk level, and trading style before copying their trades. There are no extra fees for copying — you pay the same trading costs as normal.

### 4. What platforms does FXTM support?
FXTM supports MetaTrader 4 (MT4), MetaTrader 5 (MT5), and the proprietary FXTM Trader mobile app. Both MT4 and MT5 are available for desktop, web browser, and mobile devices. The platforms support Expert Advisors for automated trading.

### 5. Does FXTM accept traders from my country?
FXTM accepts clients from most countries worldwide but does not accept residents of the United States, Canada, Japan, Iran, North Korea, Sudan, Syria, or Cuba. Availability may vary by region — check FXTM's website for the most up-to-date country eligibility list.
