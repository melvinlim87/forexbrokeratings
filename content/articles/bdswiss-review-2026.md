# BDSwiss Review 2026: Is This Offshore Broker Worth the Risk?


---

## Overview

BDSwiss is a CFD and forex broker founded in 2012, originally established with Swiss roots before relocating its primary operations to the Comoros Islands. The broker serves clients in over 180 countries and offers more than 250 trading instruments across forex, commodities, indices, stocks, and cryptocurrencies.

What makes BDSwiss notable — and controversial — is its regulatory journey. The broker voluntarily surrendered its respected CySEC license (199/13) in 2020, shifting its primary regulatory oversight to the Mwali International Services Authority (MISA) in the Comoros Islands. Today, BDSwiss also holds an Investment Dealer License from the Financial Services Commission (FSC) of Mauritius.

For traders in non-EU regions across Asia, Africa, and the Middle East, BDSwiss offers competitive trading conditions: raw spreads from 0.0 pips, leverage up to 1:2000, and a solid multi-platform ecosystem. But the shift from tier-1 European regulation to offshore oversight raises legitimate questions about safety.

In this detailed 2026 review, we'll break down BDSwiss's regulation, trading conditions, platforms, fees, and whether this broker deserves your capital.

**Quick Verdict:** BDSwiss offers aggressive trading conditions — especially for scalpers and high-leverage traders outside the EU. However, the loss of CySEC regulation and reliance on offshore licensing means reduced investor protection. Best suited for experienced traders in non-restricted jurisdictions who prioritize low costs over regulatory prestige.

### What This Means For You

**The elephant in the room:** BDSwiss voluntarily gave up its CySEC license in 2020. This means:

- **No EU access** — BDSwiss no longer serves European Union or UK clients directly
- **No ICF protection** — EU clients previously had access to the Investor Compensation Fund (up to €20,000)
- **Offshore regulation now primary** — MISA Comoros is a significantly less stringent regulator than CySEC
- **No FCA, ASIC, or other tier-1 oversight** — The broker lacks the gold-standard licenses that top-tier brokers hold

**What BDSwiss still offers:**

- ✅ Segregated client funds in separate bank accounts
- ✅ Negative balance protection for retail clients
- ✅ Company registration in Comoros (HY00423197)
- ✅ FSC Mauritius license adds a layer of legitimate regulatory oversight
- ✅ Operating since 2012 — over 14 years of track record

**Risk Level: MEDIUM-HIGH**

The MISA (Comoros) and FSC (Mauritius) regulators provide some oversight, but neither offers the investor protection depth of tier-1 regulators like the FCA, ASIC, or CySEC. If BDSwiss were to face financial difficulties, clients would have limited recourse.
