## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is IC Markets or Pepperstone better for scalping?

Both are excellent for scalping, but **IC Markets** edges ahead with tighter average spreads on major pairs (0.1 pips vs 0.2 pips on EUR/USD in raw accounts). Over thousands of trades, this difference adds up. However, **Pepperstone** has slightly faster execution speed (<30ms vs 36.5ms), which can matter for tick-level scalping strategies.

### Which broker is safer — IC Markets or Pepperstone?

**Pepperstone** has broader regulatory coverage with 6 regulators (including the prestigious FCA in the UK), compared to IC Markets' 4 regulators. However, both brokers are ASIC-regulated with segregated client funds. From a safety perspective, both are excellent, but Pepperstone's FCA license provides an additional layer of protection for clients who can open under their UK entity.

### Can I use Expert Advisors (EAs) on both brokers?

Yes, both IC Markets and Pepperstone fully support Expert Advisors on MT4, MT5, and cTrader with no restrictions on automated trading strategies. Both also offer free VPS hosting for qualifying accounts, ensuring your EAs run 24/7 without interruption.

### Which broker has lower fees?

For raw/ECN accounts, **IC Markets** offers slightly lower all-in trading costs due to tighter spreads (average 0.1 pips on EUR/USD vs 0.2 pips at Pepperstone), while commissions are identical at $3.50/lot/side. Neither broker charges deposit or withdrawal fees. However, **Pepperstone** has a $0 minimum deposit vs IC Markets' $200, which matters if you're starting small.

### Do IC Markets and Pepperstone accept US clients?

No, neither IC Markets nor Pepperstone accepts US-based clients. Both brokers are not registered with the CFTC or NFA. US residents should use CFTC-regulated brokers like OANDA, IG US, Interactive Brokers, or Forex.com.
