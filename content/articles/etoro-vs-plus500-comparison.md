### Analysis

**Plus500 wins on regulatory breadth.** With 15 licenses compared to eToro's 7, Plus500 covers more jurisdictions — particularly in Singapore (MAS), Israel (ISA), and South Africa (FSCA). For Singapore-based traders, Plus500's MAS license is a significant advantage, providing local regulatory oversight and recourse.

Plus500 is also publicly traded on the London Stock Exchange as a FTSE 250 component, meaning its financials are transparent and audited. eToro remains private (although it has explored an IPO), which means less public financial transparency.

**eToro's regulatory strength** is still solid — FCA, ASIC, and CySEC cover the major Western markets. Its Seychelles entity (FSA-S) serves international clients with higher leverage (up to 400:1).

**Regulatory history:** Both brokers have had regulatory issues. Plus500 faced a major crisis in 2015 when the FCA ordered a freeze on all UK accounts during an AML review — its market cap halved from £860M to £460M. A $29M class action was also approved in Israel (2021) over alleged trading restrictions during the Brexit referendum. eToro's regulatory history is cleaner, though it faced scrutiny after a platform crash in 2019.

**Winner: Plus500** — broader regulation, public listing, and MAS coverage for Singapore traders.

### Total Cost Analysis

Let's calculate the effective cost for a trader making 20 trades per month on EUR/USD:

**eToro:**
- Spread cost: 1.0 pip × 20 trades = 20 pips
- Withdrawal fee: $25 (assuming 1 withdrawal/month)
- Currency conversion (GBP→USD): ~0.5–1.0%
- **Estimated monthly cost: ~$220–$270**

**Plus500:**
- Spread cost: 0.8 pips × 20 trades = 16 pips
- Withdrawal fee: $0
- Currency conversion: ~0.7%
- **Estimated monthly cost: ~$160–$210**

**Winner: Plus500** — lower spreads, no withdrawal fee, and no forced currency conversion. However, Plus500's 3-month inactivity trigger is aggressive — if you take breaks from trading, those $10/month charges add up quickly.

> **Pro tip for eToro users:** The $25 withdrawal fee is one of the most complained-about fees in the industry. If you use eToro, withdraw in larger, less frequent batches to minimize this cost. Also, UK-based traders should be aware of the forced GBP→USD conversion that eToro applies.

**eToro wins for beginners and social traders.** If you're new to trading, want to learn from experienced investors, or are interested in crypto and stocks alongside forex CFDs, eToro's CopyTrader and intuitive platform are unmatched. Our recommendation: **eToro for beginners, Plus500 for intermediate CFD traders, and neither for professionals.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is eToro better than Plus500?

It depends on what you need. eToro is better for beginners and social traders thanks to its CopyTrader feature and intuitive platform. Plus500 is better for straightforward CFD trading with tighter spreads and guaranteed stop-loss orders. Plus500 also has broader regulation (15 licenses including MAS) and is publicly listed on the LSE.

### Which has lower fees — eToro or Plus500?

Plus500 generally has lower total costs. Its EUR/USD spreads start from 0.6 pips compared to eToro's 1.0 pips, and it doesn't charge the $25 withdrawal fee that eToro does. However, Plus500's 3-month inactivity fee trigger is more aggressive than eToro's 12-month trigger.

### Can I trade real stocks on eToro and Plus500?

Yes. eToro offers real stock ownership (not CFDs) in eligible jurisdictions with 3,000+ stocks. Plus500 launched "Plus500 Invest" with 2,000+ real stocks. However, for serious stock trading, dedicated platforms like Interactive Brokers or Saxo Bank offer more instruments and lower commissions.

### Which broker is safer — eToro or Plus500?

Both are safe for retail traders. Plus500 has a slight edge due to its public LSE listing (transparent financials), 15 regulatory licenses, and MAS regulation for Singapore traders. eToro is well-regulated by FCA, ASIC, and CySEC but remains a private company. Both offer negative balance protection and client fund segregation.

### Does Plus500 have copy trading?

No. Plus500 does not offer any form of social or copy trading. If copy trading is important to you, eToro's CopyTrader is the industry leader.

### Can Singapore residents use eToro?

Yes, but eToro is not regulated by MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore). Singapore residents accessing eToro are served through the Cyprus (CySEC) or Seychelles (FSA-S) entities. Plus500, by contrast, holds a MAS Capital Markets Services License (CMS 100728), providing local regulatory protection for Singapore traders.

### Which platform is better for mobile trading?

Both offer excellent mobile apps. eToro's mobile app is rated higher for its social features and CopyTrader integration. Plus500's mobile app is praised for its speed and simplicity. Both are available on iOS and Android.
