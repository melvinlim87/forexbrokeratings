Yes. Darwinex is regulated by the FCA (UK, #521888) and CySEC (Cyprus, #340/17). It was founded in 2012 by former Goldman Sachs executive Juan Colón and has operated without regulatory issues for over 12 years.

### What is a DARWIN?

A DARWIN is a regulated financial instrument that represents a specific trading strategy. When you trade on Darwinex, your strategy can become an investable DARWIN that other people can buy. You earn 20% performance fees when your DARWIN makes money for investors. DARWINs are diversified, risk-managed, and regulated as financial instruments.

### What is the minimum deposit for Darwinex?

$500 for trading accounts. DARWIN investments start at €200 per DARWIN.

### How do DARWIN fees work?

DARWINs charge two fees: a 0.5% annual management fee (charged daily proportionally) and a 20% performance fee on profits above the high-water mark. The high-water mark ensures you only get paid on genuinely new profits, not recoveries from losses.

### Can anyone become a DARWIN strategy provider?

Any registered trader on Darwinex can develop a strategy. However, becoming an investable DARWIN requires meeting Darwinex's performance, consistency, and risk criteria. Only a subset of strategies qualify for the DARWIN marketplace.
