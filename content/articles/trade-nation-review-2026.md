# Trade Nation Review 2026: Fixed Spreads, Zero Minimums — The Fair-Trading Broker?


---

## Overview

Trade Nation is a UK-based spread betting and CFD broker that was originally founded as Core Spreads in 2014. The company rebranded to Trade Nation in 2020 as part of a strategic expansion beyond its core UK spread betting offering into international CFD trading. Today, the company operates across four regulated jurisdictions: the UK (FCA), Australia (ASIC), South Africa (FSCA), and the Bahamas (SCB).

What sets Trade Nation apart from most brokers is its fixed spread model. While the vast majority of forex brokers offer variable spreads that widen during volatile market conditions — sometimes dramatically during high-impact news events — Trade Nation guarantees fixed spreads that remain constant regardless of market volatility. This means EUR/USD stays at 0.6 pips whether the market is calm or chaotic.

The broker also stands out for its zero minimum deposit requirement across all account types and its commission-free structure. All costs are built into the spread, making it one of the most beginner-friendly fee structures in the industry.

For UK residents, Trade Nation offers spread betting, which comes with significant tax advantages: no Capital Gains Tax on profits and no stamp duty. This makes it particularly attractive for UK-based traders looking for tax-efficient trading.

**Quick Verdict:** Trade Nation is an excellent choice for UK spread bettors and beginner traders who value cost predictability and simplicity. The fixed spreads, zero minimum deposit, and dual Tier-1 regulation (FCA + ASIC) make it a solid, transparent broker. However, the market maker execution model and limited product range (~560 instruments) mean it's less suitable for advanced ECN traders and those seeking diverse markets. Score: 73/100.

### Spread Betting Mechanics

For UK spread bettors, here's how it works:

- You bet a fixed amount per point of movement (e.g., £10/point on EUR/USD)
- **Profit** = Bet Size × Points Moved (correct direction)
- **Loss** = Bet Size × Points Moved (wrong direction)
- Example: £10/point on EUR/USD, market moves 50 pips in your favor = £500 profit
- **All profits are tax-free** — No Capital Gains Tax, no stamp duty

## Verdict

Trade Nation has carved out a distinct niche in the competitive retail trading market by offering one simple thing: fixed spreads with no commissions. While most brokers compete on variable spreads that can widen unpredictably during volatile market conditions, Trade Nation offers price certainty — you know exactly what your trading costs will be before you place a trade.

For UK spread bettors, the added tax advantage (no CGT, no stamp duty) makes Trade Nation particularly appealing. The zero minimum deposit and no inactivity fees remove common barriers that prevent beginners from getting started.

However, Trade Nation is not without limitations. The market maker execution model means it acts as the counterparty to your trades, creating an inherent conflict of interest that ECN brokers avoid. The limited product range (~560 instruments) can't compete with the 17,000+ at IG or 5,600+ at XTB. And the lack of ECN execution means traders seeking raw interbank spreads won't find what they're looking for here.

**Our Rating: 3.6 / 5**

Trade Nation is the "fair and square" broker — fixed spreads, no commissions, no minimums, dual Tier-1 regulation. Perfect for UK spread bettors and beginners who value simplicity and cost predictability. Less suitable for advanced traders seeking ECN access and diverse markets.
