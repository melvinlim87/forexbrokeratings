## Tips for Trading with a Small Account

### 1. Use Micro-Lots or Nano-Lots
With a small account, position sizing is everything. Trade micro-lots (0.01) or nano-lots (0.001) to keep risk per trade at 1-2% of your account. At $50 and 0.01 lot on EUR/USD ($0.10/pip), a 2% risk ($1.00) gives you a 100-pip stop loss — more than enough for most setups.

### 2. Focus on Major Pairs
Stick to EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and AUD/USD. These pairs offer the tightest spreads and best liquidity. Exotic pairs have wider spreads that disproportionately eat into small account profits.

### 3. Keep Risk at 1-2% Per Trade
Never risk more than 2% of your account on a single trade. With a $100 account, that's $2 maximum risk. This ensures you can survive a losing streak and continue trading.

### 4. Don't Chase Leverage
Just because your broker offers 500:1 leverage doesn't mean you should use it. With a small account, excessive leverage is the fastest way to blow up. Keep your effective leverage at 10:1 or less.

### 5. Focus on Learning, Not Earning
With a $50-$200 account, your primary goal should be learning, not generating income. Treat your small account as tuition — the lessons you learn about risk management, discipline, and market behavior will be worth far more than any profits you might make.

### 6. Use Demo Accounts Strategically
Every broker in this guide offers free demo accounts. Use demo to:
- Learn platform functionality
- Test new strategies risk-free
- Practice execution speed
- Understand how spreads affect your trading costs

Then transition to a small live account to experience the psychological pressure of real money trading — something demo accounts can never replicate.
