## Executive Summary

LegacyFX is a CFD broker established in **2017** and operated by **A.N. Allnew Investments Ltd**, headquartered in **Nicosia, Cyprus**. The broker holds a **CySEC license (344/17)** for European operations and also operates through a **Vanuatu VFSC entity (14579)** for offshore clients. On paper, the CySEC license provides credibility — but the reality of trading with LegacyFX tells a very different story.

**LegacyFX's spreads are among the highest in the entire industry.** The entry-level Silver account charges **3.0–4.0 pips** on EUR/USD — that's **5–6 times the industry average** of ~0.6–1.2 pips. Even the top-tier Platinum account ($25,000–$50,000 minimum deposit) still charges **1.0–2.0 pips** on EUR/USD, which most brokers offer on their free Standard accounts.

Beyond the spreads, the broker has earned widespread notoriety for **aggressive account manager practices** — with multiple verified complaints describing managers who push clients to deposit more, encourage trading without stop losses, and generate consistent losses. Users report **daily holding fees of $5–25** on open positions, and LegacyFX's industry ranking has **declined from #38 (2022) to #62 (2025)** on ForexBrokers.com.

**Quick Verdict:** While technically CySEC-regulated, LegacyFX's trading conditions are so unfavorable that the regulatory license provides little practical benefit. The extremely high spreads, aggressive sales tactics, and negative user sentiment make this one of the poorest value propositions in the forex broker market. **We cannot recommend LegacyFX for any trader profile.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is LegacyFX a legitimate broker?
Legally, yes. LegacyFX is a registered entity with CySEC license 344/17 for EU operations and VFSC registration 14579 for offshore operations. However, the extremely high trading costs and aggressive account manager practices make it a poor choice for traders.

### What is the minimum deposit at LegacyFX?
$500 for the Silver account. Gold accounts require $5,000, and Platinum accounts require $25,000–$50,000.

### Why are LegacyFX spreads so high?
LegacyFX does not operate as a typical ECN/STP broker. Their business model relies on wide spreads as the primary revenue source. Even Platinum account spreads remain significantly above industry average.

### Should I follow my LegacyFX account manager's trading advice?
**Caution advised.** Multiple verified reviews indicate that account managers are incentivized to generate trading volume rather than client profitability. Users consistently report that account manager signals lead to losses. Conduct your own analysis and never trade without stop losses.

### Can I withdraw money from LegacyFX?
Withdrawals are processed, but multiple users report delays and high fees. Some users report technical issues with the withdrawal section of the website.

### Is LegacyFX regulated by CySEC?
Yes, A.N. Allnew Investments Ltd holds CySEC license 344/17. However, CySEC regulation only applies to EU-resident clients. Global clients are directed to the Vanuatu entity with weaker protections.

### Does LegacyFX offer MetaTrader 4?
No, LegacyFX only offers MetaTrader 5 (MT5). If you require MT4, you'll need to look elsewhere.

### What are the daily holding fees at LegacyFX?
Users report daily holding fees of $5–25 per position. On a position held for 30 days, this could add $150–750 in hidden costs.

### Why has LegacyFX's industry ranking declined?
The decline from #38 (2022) to #62 (2025) is attributed to consistently high trading costs, negative user feedback, limited research tools, and an overall poor value proposition.
