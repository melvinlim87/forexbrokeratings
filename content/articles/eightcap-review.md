## Regulation & Trust — Is Eightcap Safe?

Regulation should always be your first consideration when evaluating a broker. Eightcap holds licenses from two regulatory bodies:

| Regulator | License Number | Region |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **ASIC** (Australian Securities & Investments Commission) | AFSL 391441 | Australia |
| **SCB** (Securities Commission of The Bahamas) | SIA-F220 | Bahamas |

### What This Means For You

- **ASIC regulation** is one of the most stringent in the world. ASIC requires brokers to maintain adequate capital, submit regular audits, and comply with strict conduct standards. Australian clients benefit from the highest level of regulatory protection.
- **SCB regulation** allows Eightcap to offer higher leverage (up to 500:1) and serve international clients who may not qualify under ASIC's stricter retail protections.
- **Negative balance protection** is provided, ensuring you can never lose more than your account balance.
- **Segregated client funds** are held in tier-1 Australian banks, separate from Eightcap's operational funds.

Eightcap has operated since 2009 without any major regulatory actions, enforcement orders, or public scandals. The broker's longevity and clean regulatory track record contribute positively to its trustworthiness. However, it's worth noting that SCB (offshore) regulation does not offer the same level of investor protection as ASIC — this is standard across the industry for offshore entities.

### Trust Score Assessment

Eightcap earns a solid trust rating thanks to its ASIC license, long operating history, and clean compliance record. It may not have the multi-jurisdiction depth of brokers like Pepperstone (which holds ASIC, FCA, CySEC, and DFSA licenses), but its dual regulation is sufficient for most retail traders outside the EU and UK.
