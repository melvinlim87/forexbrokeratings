## Overview

Dukascopy occupies a rare and respected position in the retail forex industry. Founded in 2004 in Geneva, Switzerland, by Andre and Veronika Duka, the company obtained its Swiss banking license from FINMA in 2010 — making it one of the very few brokers in the world that operates as a fully licensed Swiss bank.

What sets Dukascopy apart is its proprietary trading infrastructure. The SWFX (Swiss Forex Marketplace), launched in 2006, is a true ECN platform that aggregates liquidity from multiple banks and financial institutions. Unlike MetaTrader-based brokers that bridge to external liquidity pools, Dukascopy operates its own exchange-like marketplace with full order book visibility and no dealing desk intervention.

As a Swiss bank, Dukascopy offers more than just trading. Clients get multi-currency bank accounts, Visa debit cards, e-banking, and even cryptocurrency services — all under the umbrella of Swiss banking regulation.

The trade-offs are real: a higher minimum deposit ($1,000), lower leverage under Swiss and EU regulations (1:30–1:100), and a proprietary platform (JForex) that has a steeper learning curve than MT4/MT5. But for traders who prioritize regulatory safety and execution quality, Dukascopy is in a class of its own.

**Quick Verdict:** Dukascopy is highly recommended for serious forex traders, algorithmic traders, and safety-conscious investors who value Swiss banking regulation over high leverage. It earns an 82/100 rating — one of the highest scores we give — held back only by its higher barrier to entry and lower leverage limits.
