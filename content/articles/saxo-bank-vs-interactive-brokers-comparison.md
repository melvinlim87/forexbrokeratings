### Analysis

This is the closest regulation matchup in the brokerage industry. Both brokers hold 8+ Tier 1 licenses, but their advantages are complementary.

**Saxo Bank's unique advantages:**
- **Full Danish banking license** — the highest level of financial authorization. Saxo can accept deposits, issue loans, and provide full banking services. Most forex brokers only hold investment firm licenses.
- **SIFI classification** — since June 2023, the Danish FSA considers Saxo a Systemically Important Financial Institution. Only 5 other major Danish banks hold this status. SIFI status subjects Saxo to higher capital reserves (SIFI buffer) and enhanced supervision.
- **Danish DGS deposit protection** — up to EUR 100,000 for cash deposits and EUR 20,000 for investment losses.
- **Swiss banking license (FINMA)** — providing banking-grade regulation in Switzerland.

**Interactive Brokers' unique advantages:**
- **SEC regulation** — the gold standard of financial regulation globally. Direct SEC oversight is the most rigorous supervisory framework in the world.
- **SIPC protection** — up to $500,000 (including $250,000 for cash) plus Lloyd's of London excess SIPC up to $30 million per account (aggregate $150 million).
- **CFTC/NFA oversight** — allows IBKR to offer forex and futures to US residents (very few brokers have this).
- **FINRA membership** — additional investor protection and regulatory oversight.
- **Public NASDAQ listing** — $70 billion market cap provides maximum financial transparency. Quarterly earnings reports, audited financials, and full SEC disclosure.

**Regulatory history:** Both brokers have clean regulatory records. Saxo Bank has operated for 30+ years without major enforcement actions. Interactive Brokers has 47+ years of operation with no significant regulatory issues.

**Winner: Tie** — Saxo has banking-grade protection and SIFI status; IBKR has SEC oversight and SIPC/Lloyd's insurance. Both represent the pinnacle of broker regulation.

Interactive Brokers is significantly cheaper. An active forex trader can save approximately $3,288 per year by choosing IBKR Pro over Saxo Classic, based on 50 EUR/USD standard lot trades per month. IBKR's all-in cost of 0.65 pips is roughly 50% lower than Saxo Classic's 1.1 pips spread.

Yes. Interactive Brokers is one of the safest brokers in the world for large accounts. It is publicly traded on NASDAQ (~$70B market cap), regulated by the SEC/CFTC/NFA, and provides SIPC protection up to $500,000 plus Lloyd's of London excess SIPC coverage up to $30 million per account.

### Which has better platforms — Saxo or IBKR?
