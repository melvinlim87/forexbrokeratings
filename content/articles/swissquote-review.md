## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is Swissquote really a bank?
Yes. Swissquote is a fully licensed Swiss bank regulated by FINMA. It holds a full banking licence (not just a broker licence), which means it's subject to the same capital requirements and prudential regulations as UBS, Credit Suisse (now UBS), and other Swiss banks. It's also publicly listed on the SIX Swiss Exchange (ticker: SQN).

### 2. What is the minimum deposit for Swissquote?
The minimum deposit for a standard trading account is $1,000 (or CHF 1,000). Premium accounts require $10,000+, and Prime accounts require $50,000+. For a savings account (non-trading), there is no minimum.

### 3. Can I buy real stocks on Swissquote?
Yes. Swissquote offers direct market access (DMA) to 3 million+ instruments including real stocks, ETFs, bonds, and options on major exchanges worldwide. Unlike CFD-only brokers, you actually own the underlying assets when you buy stocks through Swissquote.

### 4. Does Swissquote offer crypto trading?
Yes. Swissquote offers both crypto CFDs (for leveraged trading) and actual cryptocurrency buying/selling with institutional-grade custody. They support 30+ cryptocurrencies and allow trading against CHF, EUR, USD, and BTC. The custody service uses cold storage in Swiss military bunkers.

### 5. How does Swissquote protect my funds?
As a Swiss bank, deposits up to CHF 100,000 are protected by esisuisse (Swiss deposit insurance). Additionally, Swissquote carries excess insurance of CHF 1 million. Client funds are held in segregated accounts, and as a FINMA-regulated bank, Swissquote must maintain higher capital reserves than most broker-only entities.
